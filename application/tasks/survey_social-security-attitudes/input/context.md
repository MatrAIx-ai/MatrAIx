# Social Security Attitudes

Attitudes toward Social Security — knowledge of the program, reliance on it, claiming decisions, and views on its future.

## About this survey

This survey identifies the respondent's relationship to Social Security and measures self-rated understanding, expected importance and share of retirement income, knowledge of their benefit amount, and whether they have checked their ssa.gov statement. It captures claiming plans and the factors driving them, along with confidence that full benefits will be paid. Likert batteries assess attitudes about the program as a promise, worry about its future, dependence on benefits, and understanding of claiming options, and a support/oppose matrix rates reform options such as raising the payroll tax, raising the retirement age, lifting the earnings cap, and reducing benefits. Further items cover trust in government, program-solvency worry, other income sources, effects on personal saving, information sources, and generational fairness. Outcome questions include a top-three ranking of what people want from Social Security, perceived importance, satisfaction, and likelihood of seeking advice, with a free-text item for planned claiming age. A full demographic block records age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urbanicity, and political outlook.
