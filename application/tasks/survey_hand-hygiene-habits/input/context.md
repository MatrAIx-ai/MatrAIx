# Hand Hygiene Habits

Everyday hand-washing and hand-sanitizing habits, products, and attitudes.

## About this survey

This survey measures how often respondents wash their hands, in which situations, and what they use (soap and water, sanitizer, wipes), along with typical wash duration and whether they carry sanitizer. It captures products kept on hand, features sought when buying soap or sanitizer, and how habits have changed over recent years. An agreement-scale attitude battery covers diligence, belief in illness prevention, dryness from washing, sanitizer as a substitute, and noticing others' behavior, alongside perceived importance, lotion use, seasonal care, barriers, comfort with public facilities, home and public drying methods, reminders, belief sources, a top-three situations ranking, satisfaction, and future intent, plus open comments. Numeric free-text captures a yesterday wash count and household size. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, employment, household composition and size, income, region, and urban or rural setting. Content is framed attitudinally and kept consumer-oriented.
