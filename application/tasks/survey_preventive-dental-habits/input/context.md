# Preventive Dental Habits

Everyday preventive dental care habits, routines, products, and attitudes.

## About this survey

This survey measures respondents' daily oral-care behavior, including brushing frequency and duration, flossing, toothbrush type, and the preventive products and toothpaste features they use. It captures checkup cadence and recency, preventive services received, what drives dental-visit frequency, dental coverage, brush-replacement habits, self-rated dental health, whitening use, and smart-toothbrush or reminder app use. An agreement-scale attitude battery covers self-care, the value of prevention, dental anxiety, confidence in technique, and cost as a barrier, alongside the importance of a white smile, sugar-limiting behavior, information sources, a top-three habit ranking, views on cost, monthly product spend, satisfaction, and maintenance and improvement intent, plus open comments. Numeric free-text captures brushing time, product spend, and household size. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, employment, income, marital status, household size, region, and urban or rural setting. Content is framed attitudinally and kept consumer-oriented.
