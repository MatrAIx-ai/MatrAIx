# Workplace Wellness Programs

Employee experience with, participation in, and attitudes toward employer-sponsored wellness programs.

## About this survey

This survey begins by screening for employment, employer size, and whether a wellness program is offered, then inventories the specific offerings available and which ones the respondent has personally used. It measures how people learned about the program, financial incentives and their dollar value, and the motivators and barriers that drive or block participation, along with comfort sharing health data. Attitudinal Likert batteries cover program intent, convenience, data privacy, health impact, and manager encouragement, and a rated matrix battery scores the perceived value of fitness, mental health, screening, financial, and nutrition offerings. Later sections capture lasting behavior change, satisfaction, retention influence, future participation, a top-3 ranking of desired additions, and a 0–10 recommendation score. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert and value scales, a matrix battery, and numeric free-text, followed by a full demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, industry, relationship status, household size, region, urbanicity, and income.
