# Telemedicine with Specialists

Consumer use of, experience with, and attitudes toward seeing medical specialists through telehealth.

## About this survey

This survey measures whether and how recently people have had a telehealth visit with a specialist (rather than primary care), which specialties (dermatology, cardiology, mental health, endocrinology, and others), and how the visit was conducted, connected, and arranged, including platform used and appointment wait time. It captures reasons for choosing telehealth, whether it replaced or added to in-person care, cost and insurance coverage, and drawbacks. Attitudes are captured through agreement and satisfaction Likert batteries covering care quality, technical issues, access, comfort, and privacy, plus an importance battery, a "top 3" decision-factors ranking, willingness for future use across scenarios, overall satisfaction, and a 0–10 recommendation scale. Numeric free-text items capture number of visits, visit length, distance to the in-person office, and household size, followed by a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, marital status, household size, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural, and insurance type).
