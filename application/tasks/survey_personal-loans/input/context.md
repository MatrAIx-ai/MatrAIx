# Personal Loans

How consumers use, shop for, and manage unsecured personal installment loans.

## About this survey

This survey explores the personal loan experience from whether and why someone borrows through the specifics of their most recent loan — amount, term, APR, monthly payment, lender source, and whether it was secured. It measures shopping behavior (prequalification, comparing offers, alternatives considered), repayment habits, and credit standing, then uses agreement and importance Likert batteries to gauge attitudes about the decision, the process, and repayment worries. Sub-topics include debt consolidation outcomes, likelihood of borrowing again, and exposure to prequalified offers, followed by satisfaction, a 0-to-10 recommendation score, a top-3 selection-factors ranking, a confidence rating, and an optional comment. A comprehensive demographic block records age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural setting, and homeownership.
