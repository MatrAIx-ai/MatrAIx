# Teen Banking

How teenagers use banking products, debit cards, and money-management apps, from the perspective of their parents and guardians.

## About this survey

This survey is answered by parents and guardians of teenagers. It screens for having a teen aged 13–18, then captures whether the teen has an account or money app, the products and providers used (from traditional banks and credit unions to Greenlight, GoHenry, and Cash App), how the teen gets money, and how much they spend. Behavioral items cover card usage, parental oversight, and the features that matter most when choosing a product. Likert batteries assess attitudes about learning, overspending worry, fees, and privacy, and an importance matrix rates money skills parents want their teen to build (budgeting, saving, needs versus wants, card use, and understanding credit). Additional items address money conversations, savings goals, perceived responsibility, where teens learn about money, a top-three skills ranking, key concerns, and satisfaction and recommendation for the product used, with free-text items for the age an account was opened and monthly spending money. A full demographic block records the parent's age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, number of children, marital status, region, urbanicity, and financial knowledge.
