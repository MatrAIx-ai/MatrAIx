# Vision Care

Consumer vision care habits, including eye exams, glasses and contacts, and how people shop for and afford eyewear.

## About this survey

This survey measures whether respondents use vision correction, how recently and often they get eye exams, where they get exams and buy eyewear, and what they spend and how they pay. It captures the factors that drive eyewear purchases, Likert batteries on attitudes toward exams, cost, and online buying, and a matrix rating what matters when choosing where to get vision care. It also covers cost-related delays, screen-related eye strain and protective habits, satisfaction with current eyewear, confidence in one's prescription, self-rated vision, likelihood of a future exam, overall satisfaction, a likelihood-to-recommend scale, and a top-3 ranking of improvements. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, a matrix battery split per item, numeric free-text items, and a demographic block covering age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, area type, vision coverage, and self-rated health.
