# Financial Goal Setting

How people set, track, and make progress toward their personal financial goals, and how confident they feel about reaching them.

## About this survey

This survey screens whether respondents have defined financial goals and how they track them, then captures the number and mix of goals, the single most important goal, its time horizon and dollar target, and the methods used to make progress. It records how often people review progress and whether they set measurable targets. Attitudes are measured through agreement batteries on the value of goal-setting, sense of control, follow-through, derailment by surprises, and the power of tracking, plus a confidence battery across emergency funds, debt payoff, retirement, major purchases, and budgeting. Additional items cover the biggest obstacle, tools and support used, a top-3 motivations ranking, goals achieved in the past year, satisfaction, intent to set new goals, and a 0-10 on-track scale, along with open numeric and text fields describing the primary goal. It ends with a demographic block including age, gender, race or ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, community type, and perceived financial situation.
