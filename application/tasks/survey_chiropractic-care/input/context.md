# Chiropractic Care

Consumer experiences with and attitudes toward chiropractic care.

## About this survey

This survey covers whether respondents have seen a chiropractor, what first led them to try it, the concerns they seek care for, how often they go, how long they have been going, how they found their chiropractor, how care is paid for, out-of-pocket cost per visit, and yearly visit count. It measures the services their chiropractor provides (adjustments, soft-tissue work, exercise guidance, posture advice, modalities), other approaches they combine, and scheduling ease. It runs Likert batteries on attitudes (relief, clear explanation, value for money, trust, preferring it over medication, wishing for more insurance coverage) and on the importance of factors in choosing a chiropractor, plus a top-3 ranking of those factors, the main goal for care and how well it is met, barriers, comfort during visits, fit with routine, confidence, satisfaction, intent to continue, a 0–10 recommendation score, and an open comment. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, a matrix battery split per item, a top-3 ranking, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured: age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urban/rural, insurance status, and physical demands of a typical day.
