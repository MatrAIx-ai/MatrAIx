# Tablet Usage

How consumers own, use, and evaluate tablet computers such as iPads, Galaxy Tabs, and Fire tablets.

## About this survey

This survey measures tablet ownership and usage across the consumer population, beginning with a screener on whether the respondent uses a tablet and moving into brand, operating system, screen size, accessories, and where and how tablets are used. It captures attitudes through several Likert batteries — agreement statements about the role of the tablet in the respondent's device ecosystem and importance ratings for purchase factors — plus a top-three ranking of buying priorities. Sub-topic sections drill into streaming, reading, children's use, and the devices tablets have displaced, followed by satisfaction, recommendation likelihood, and future purchase intent. The instrument uses single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, a per-item importance matrix, a ranking question, and numeric free-text items. It closes with a comprehensive demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urbanicity, presence of children, and technology-adoption profile.
