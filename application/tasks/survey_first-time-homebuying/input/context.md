# First-Time Homebuying

The experiences, plans, and challenges of buying a first home.

## About this survey

This survey captures where respondents are in the first-home journey — recent buyers, active shoppers, or aspiring owners — along with home type sought, motivations, down payment sources and size, and mortgage type (conventional, FHA, VA, USDA, or first-time buyer programs). It measures pre-approval status, resources used such as agents and listing sites like Zillow and Redfin, obstacles, and challenges faced. Likert batteries gauge attitudes toward homeownership, feeling overwhelmed, affordability, and renting, plus the importance of price, schools, location, space, and condition, while multi-select and top-3 items cover reasons, funding, resources, challenges, and priorities. A 0–10 optimism scale and numeric free-text record target price, savings, and income, and a full demographic block captures age, gender, race or ethnicity, income, education, employment, self-reported credit score range, household size, marital status, region, and community type.
