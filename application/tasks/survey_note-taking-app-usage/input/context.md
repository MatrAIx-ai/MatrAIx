# Note-Taking App Usage

Which note-taking apps people use, how they capture and organize notes across devices, and what they value in a note system.

## About this survey

This survey captures a person's primary and recently used note apps (Apple Notes, Google Keep, OneNote, Notion, Evernote, Obsidian, plain text, and paper), how long they've used them, note volume, purposes, and context. It measures capture methods, organization approaches, revisit behavior, devices, syncing, and advanced feature use, then applies Likert agreement and importance batteries plus a top-3 feature ranking to gauge attitudes toward organization, findability, over-capture, lock-in, and system-tinkering. Later items address switching history, AI note features, payment, privacy concern, paper use, satisfaction, future intent, and a likelihood-to-recommend score, with free-text on years of use, note count, and desired improvements. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, relationship status, U.S. region, urban/rural setting, occupation, and self-rated technical skill.
