# Hydration App Usage

Consumer use of water-tracking and hydration reminder apps.

## About this survey

This survey measures whether respondents use a hydration or water-tracking app, how long they have used one, which kind and platform (standalone app, feature in a broader app, smart bottle, phone reminders), and why they started. It captures logging frequency and method, typical daily intake, personal goals, and the features they value, plus a top-three feature ranking. An agreement-scale attitude battery covers whether the app increased water intake, routine fit, reminder annoyance, hydration awareness, and reliance on the app, alongside gamification motivation, wearable and smart-bottle connections, paid features, importance of being free, barriers, data-privacy concern, churn, satisfaction, a 0–10 recommendation scale, renewal intent, and open comments. Numeric free-text captures daily cups and a personal goal. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, employment, income, region, urban or rural setting, and overall health-and-fitness-tracking interest. Content is framed attitudinally and kept consumer-oriented.
