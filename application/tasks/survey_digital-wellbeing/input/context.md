# Digital Wellbeing & Screen Habits

People's screen-time habits, use of digital wellbeing tools, and their broader relationship with technology.

## About this survey

This survey measures how much time people spend on screens, which devices and activities dominate that time, and which wellbeing features and app blockers they use to manage it. It covers behaviors such as morning and bedtime phone use, overnight device placement, digital detoxes, and strategies people try to cut back. It uses agreement-scale Likert batteries on control and mood, a per-item frequency matrix on how screens interfere with sleep, work, relationships, and activity, a "top 3 goals" ranking, and numeric free-text items on desired screen time and daily phone pickups. It closes with satisfaction, future intent, a 0-10 balance scale, and a full demographics block including age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household, children, region, locale, and screen-based work intensity. Content is framed attitudinally and remains consumer-oriented.
