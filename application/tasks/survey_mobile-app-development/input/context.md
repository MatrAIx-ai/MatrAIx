# Mobile App Development

How people build mobile apps: the platforms, frameworks, tooling, distribution, and practices they use and their satisfaction with them.

## About this survey

This survey profiles the respondent's mobile-development role and experience, then maps target platforms, native vs. cross-platform approach, and the frameworks and languages they use (Swift/SwiftUI, Kotlin/Jetpack Compose, Flutter, React Native, .NET MAUI, Ionic, Kotlin Multiplatform, and more), preferred cross-platform choice, IDEs, and backends. It measures testing, distribution channels, monetization, release cadence, CI/CD, analytics/monitoring tools, and weekly hours, followed by Likert agreement statements, a challenge-rating matrix and a satisfaction matrix split per item, and AI-tool usage. It closes with a top-3 approach-factors ranking, a likelihood-to-recommend score, overall satisfaction, framework-switch intent, and an open item. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, matrices split per item, a ranking, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured include age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, years of professional experience, company size, work arrangement, U.S. region, and urban/suburban/rural.
