# Estate Planning

Adults' attitudes toward and experiences with estate planning.

## About this survey

This survey covers which estate planning documents people hold (wills, trusts, powers of attorney, healthcare proxies, advance directives, beneficiary designations), when they were created and last updated, and how they were prepared, whether with an attorney, an online service, or a financial advisor. It measures the assets a plan must address, whether executors and decision-makers are named, family conversations, and the barriers that stop people from planning. Likert and importance batteries capture peace of mind, misconceptions, knowledge, and the goals people weigh such as directing assets, avoiding probate, minimizing taxes, and providing for spouses, children, or charity, plus a ranked set of planning priorities. Closing items probe guardianship, expected costs, confidence, satisfaction, and intent to act. A full demographic block records age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, net worth, education, employment, marital status, homeownership, region, and urbanicity.
