# Wire Transfer Usage

How people send and receive wire transfers and other large money transfers, and how they handle fees and fraud risk.

## About this survey

This survey screens for sending and receiving wires and large transfers and captures which methods are used (domestic and international bank wires, money-transfer services like Western Union and MoneyGram, online services like Wise and Remitly, and peer-to-peer apps). It records what transfers are used for, how the most recent one was sent, amounts, fees, counts, and whether it was domestic or international. Agreement and importance batteries measure perceptions of fees, ease, fraud risk, speed, and trust, including a matrix on what matters when sending, plus a top-3 ranking of selection factors. Additional items cover fee comparison shopping, fraud concern and exposure, safety steps taken, delivery time, and problems. It closes with future likelihood, satisfaction, a likelihood-to-recommend score, and an open comment. A comprehensive demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, immigrant/remittance status, household size, marital status, region, and area type so personas can be matched.
