# College Savings (529 Plans)

How families save for future education costs, their use of 529 plans and other accounts, and their attitudes toward college saving.

## About this survey

This survey screens whether the respondent saves for education and holds a 529 plan, then captures who they save for, the number of beneficiaries, the account types used (529 savings and prepaid, Coverdell ESA, custodial, brokerage, savings bonds), amounts saved and contributed, contribution methods, providers, and saving motivations. Likert batteries measure priority, understanding of 529 tax benefits, worry about shortfalls, retirement trade-offs, and confidence; an importance battery rates account-selection criteria; and a "top 3" ranking identifies top concerns. Additional questions cover how funds are invested, state tax benefits, on-track feelings, expected funding sources beyond savings, information sources, recent changes in saving, stress, satisfaction, contribution intent, and a 0–10 recommendation scale. It closes with a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, number of children, region, and urban/rural).
