# Digital Banking Adoption

How consumers adopt and use online and mobile banking, the features they rely on, and their attitudes toward digital versus in-branch banking.

## About this survey

This survey screens for digital banking use and tenure, then captures where respondents bank, access frequency, primary device, and the tasks and features they use digitally, including mobile deposit, alerts, bill pay, and card controls. It measures branch visits, decision factors through an importance battery (app quality, branches, fees, security, support), and attitudes via agreement scales about ease, security worries, in-person preference, and branchless comfort. Additional sections cover security methods, phishing confidence, past problems, a top-3 ranking of benefits, hesitations, changes in branch use, satisfaction with digital services and the app, future intent, and a likelihood-to-recommend scale, with numeric free-text for weekly logins and installed apps. A comprehensive demographics block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, community type, technology comfort, home internet, and smartphone type.
