# Music Streaming Services

How people listen to music through streaming services, including which services and plans they use, listening habits, and what they value in a service.

## About this survey

This survey covers music streaming behavior: which services respondents use, their main service and plan type, monthly spend, daily listening time, devices, and listening contexts. It measures music discovery methods and engagement behaviors (playlists, saving songs, offline downloads, sharing) through a frequency battery, and attitudes toward value, recommendations, routine, audio quality, and switching through Likert scales. An importance battery covers desired features, and a top-3 ranking captures favorite genres, alongside service switching, ad tolerance, ownership of physical media, views on artist pay, recap features, and future spending intent. The question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert and importance batteries, a ranking question, a 0–10 recommendation scale, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, U.S. region, and urban/suburban/rural setting.
