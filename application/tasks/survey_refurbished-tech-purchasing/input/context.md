# Refurbished & Used Tech Purchasing

How consumers approach buying refurbished, certified pre-owned, and used technology — the categories, marketplaces, trust, and trade-offs involved.

## About this survey

This survey measures whether and how often people buy refurbished instead of new, which device categories they buy used, where they shop (manufacturer certified stores, Amazon Renewed, Back Market, eBay, Swappa, local marketplaces), and which sources they trust. It captures preferred condition grades, motivations, concerns, the discount that makes used worthwhile, payment methods, and selling or trade-in behavior. Likert agreement and buying-factor importance batteries gauge attitudes toward savings, reliability, e-waste, and risk, and a ranking item surfaces the top decision drivers. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, split matrix batteries, a top-3 ranking, numeric and open free-text, plus satisfaction, recommendation, and future-purchase-intent items, followed by a full demographic block covering age, gender, race/ethnicity, education, employment, tech-consumer type, income, household, relationship status, region, and area type.
