# Cloud Gaming

How people perceive and use cloud gaming services that stream games from remote servers, including adoption, performance experience, and attitudes.

## About this survey

This survey covers cloud gaming: respondents' familiarity and adoption, which services they have used, devices, and weekly hours. It measures what attracts people to cloud gaming, a top-3 ranking of concerns, home internet type and speed, and performance issues (input lag, video quality, disconnections, queues) through a frequency battery. Attitudes toward access, performance adequacy, ownership, the future of gaming, and cost savings are measured through Likert scales, and an importance battery covers what people value in a service, alongside subscription spending, how cloud gaming fits alongside other play, likelihood of future use, satisfaction, and intent. The question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert and importance batteries, matrix-style frequency items, a ranking question, a 0–10 recommendation scale, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, U.S. region, and urban/suburban/rural setting.
