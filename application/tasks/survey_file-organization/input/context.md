# File Organization

How people organize, name, store, and find their digital files and folders.

## About this survey

This survey opens with a screener on the respondent's overall organization state, how they find files, how often they tidy up, and whether they follow a consistent structure, then details where files live, which cloud and sync services they use, the organizing strategies and naming approaches they follow, how they handle Downloads, and which search or automation tools they rely on. Attitudes are measured with an agreement battery (findability, desktop clutter, duplicates, naming consistency, search reliance, stress, and deletion habits), a folders-versus-search spectrum item, an importance matrix for what makes a good file system, and a top-three ranking of pain points, plus reorganization triggers and backup frequency. Outcome items cover satisfaction, confidence in finding an old file, adoption intent, and likelihood to recommend, using single-choice, multi-select, Likert, matrix, ranking, and numeric free-text formats. The demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, relationship status, region, urban/suburban/rural setting, computer role, and overall tech comfort.
