# Physical Activity Habits

Consumer physical activity habits, including exercise types, frequency, motivations, and the barriers people face.

## About this survey

This survey measures respondents' overall activity level, how many days and minutes they exercise, the types of activity they do, and where and when they do it. It captures the tools and supports they use, their motivations, Likert batteries on attitudes toward exercise (priority, time, enjoyment, motivation, satisfaction), and a matrix rating how much barriers such as time, motivation, cost, fatigue, and place affect them. It also covers sitting time, change over the past year, goal-setting, mood benefits, confidence in staying active, importance to well-being, likelihood of increasing activity, overall satisfaction, a likelihood-to-recommend scale, and a top-3 ranking of what would help. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, a matrix battery split per item, numeric free-text items, and a demographic block covering age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, area type, work activity level, and self-rated health.
