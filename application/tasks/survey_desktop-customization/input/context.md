# Desktop Customization

How people personalize and customize their computer desktop environment.

## About this survey

This survey opens with a screener on how heavily the respondent customizes, which OS they personalize, how often they change their setup, and how important personalization is, then details what they customize (wallpaper, themes, dock, icons, widgets, window management, shortcuts, terminal), the tools they use (Rainmeter, PowerToys, Wallpaper Engine, Bartender, Raycast, tiling window managers, desktop-environment extensions), their wallpaper and appearance-mode preferences, and where they find ideas. Attitudes are measured with an agreement battery (productivity, enjoyment, waste-of-time, self-expression, setup envy, stock-defaults preference, and minimalism), an aesthetics-versus-function spectrum item, an importance matrix for customization aspects, and a top-three ranking of most-customized elements, plus reasons to customize, dotfile syncing, and desktop tidiness. Outcome items cover satisfaction, personality fit, overhaul intent, and likelihood to recommend, using single-choice, multi-select, Likert, matrix, ranking, and numeric free-text formats. The demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, relationship status, region, urban/suburban/rural setting, technical role, and overall tech comfort.
