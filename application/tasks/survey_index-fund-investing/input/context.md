# Index Fund Investing

How investors use low-cost index funds and ETFs to build their portfolios.

## About this survey

This survey covers index fund investing from adoption and tenure through the specific fund types (S&P 500, total market, international, bond, target-date), fund families (Vanguard, Fidelity, Schwab, iShares, SPDR), and accounts used, along with amounts invested, monthly contributions, funding method, expense-ratio awareness, and rebalancing habits. It uses agreement and importance Likert batteries to capture beliefs about low cost, passive versus active investing, simplicity, and crash worries, and to rate the factors people weigh when picking a fund. Sub-topics include dollar-cost averaging, behavior during market drops, and other holdings, followed by confidence, satisfaction with returns, likelihood of continued investing, a 0-to-10 recommendation score, a top-3 reasons ranking, and an optional comment. A full demographic block records age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural setting, and investing experience.
