# Real Estate Investing

How people invest in real estate, such as rental properties, flips, and property deals.

## About this survey

This survey screens for owning investment real estate (excluding a primary residence) and captures tenure, property types (single- and multi-family rentals, short-term rentals, flips, commercial, land, crowdfunding, syndications), number of properties, portfolio value, and rental income. It records investing strategy, how deals are financed, and how properties are managed, along with reasons for investing. Agreement and concern batteries measure attitudes on wealth building, tax benefits, landlord workload, and risk, including a matrix of specific risks (vacancies, repairs, tenants, values, rates, taxes) and a top-3 ranking of deal-evaluation factors. Additional items cover information sources, risk tolerance, deal-hunting frequency, and confidence. It closes with changing activity, satisfaction, forward plans, a likelihood-to-recommend score, and an open comment. A comprehensive demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, primary-home ownership, household size, marital status, region, and area type so personas can be matched.
