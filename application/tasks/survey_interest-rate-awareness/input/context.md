# Interest Rate Awareness

How aware people are of interest rates on their savings and loans and how rate changes shape their financial decisions.

## About this survey

This survey measures how closely people follow Federal Reserve and interest-rate news, their sense of which direction rates have moved, and their self-rated understanding of how rates work. It captures concrete knowledge — whether respondents know the APY on their savings and the APR on their credit cards (with a free-text rate estimate) — and which rate-sensitive products they hold. Behavioral items cover rate shopping, moving money for better yields, information sources, and actions taken in response to recent rate changes. Likert batteries assess attitudes toward saving, borrowing, and the complexity of rate news, and a matrix battery rates how well respondents understand rates' effects on savings, credit cards, loan payments, borrowing costs, and investments. Further questions address variable-rate debt, switching likelihood, a top-three ranking of decisions where rates matter most, and satisfaction with current yields. A full demographic block records age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urbanicity, homeownership, and financial comfort.
