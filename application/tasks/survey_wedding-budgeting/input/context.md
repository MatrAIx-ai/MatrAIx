# Wedding Budgeting

How couples plan, budget for, and pay for a wedding, and their attitudes toward wedding spending.

## About this survey

This survey identifies the respondent's relationship to wedding planning and captures the total budget, guest count, who is paying, the biggest expense categories, and how the wedding is funded (savings, family contributions, credit, loans, buy-now-pay-later, and cash gifts). Likert batteries measure attitudes toward wedding costs, social pressure, debt, and vendor pricing; an importance battery rates individual wedding elements; and a "top 3" ranking captures splurge priorities alongside a checklist of money-saving strategies and planning resources. Additional questions address going over budget, wedding debt, budgeting stress, tension with partners or families, the impact of inflation, feelings about the amount spent, satisfaction, and confidence that the spending is worthwhile. It closes with a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, relationship status, region, urban/rural, and self-rated financial situation).
