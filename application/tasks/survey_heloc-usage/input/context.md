# Home Equity Borrowing (HELOC)

How homeowners use, view, and decide about borrowing against their home equity through HELOCs, home equity loans, and cash-out refinances.

## About this survey

This survey screens for homeownership and prior or prospective use of home equity products, distinguishing HELOCs, home equity loans, and cash-out refinances. It collects the respondent's estimated equity, credit limit, amount borrowed, rate type, purposes for borrowing (renovations, debt consolidation, education, medical, investing, emergencies), the main reason chosen, and lenders considered. Likert batteries measure attitudes toward cost, risk to the home, understanding of terms, and rate sensitivity; an importance battery rates product-selection criteria; and a "top 3" ranking captures top concerns. Additional questions address shopping behavior, comfort with added debt, draw-period utilization, repayment confidence, how changing rates and home values have shifted views, satisfaction, borrowing intent, and a 0–10 recommendation scale. It closes with a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, years of homeownership, region, and urban/rural).
