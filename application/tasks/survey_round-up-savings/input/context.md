# Round-Up Savings

How people use round-up savings and investing features that automatically set aside the spare change from everyday purchases.

## About this survey

This survey screens for awareness and use of round-up savings features, how long respondents have used them, and which apps and services they rely on (such as Acorns, Chime, Qapital, Stash, and bank round-up programs). It captures behaviors including where round-ups are directed, use of multipliers, additional contributions, how often balances are checked, and savings goals, along with free-text amounts for monthly round-ups, accumulated balance, and typical checking balance. Likert batteries measure attitudes toward automated saving, whether small amounts are noticed, overdraft worries, and sense of control, plus an importance matrix on tool features (fees, returns, access, goals, multipliers) and a top-3 ranking of reasons for use. Further items probe fees, concerns, reasons for pausing, other automatic savings methods, satisfaction, likelihood to continue, a 0–10 recommendation score, and an optional open comment. A full demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, education, employment, income, marital status, household size, region, urbanicity, and comfort with financial apps.
