# Checking Account Usage

How people choose, use, and feel about their everyday checking accounts.

## About this survey

This survey covers where respondents hold their primary checking account — national banks like Chase and Bank of America, credit unions, or neobanks like Chime and Ally — and how they access and use it, including mobile deposit, bill pay, Zelle, debit cards, and direct deposit. It measures transaction volume, balances, fees paid, overdraft frequency, branch, check, and ATM usage, and how the account was chosen. Likert batteries capture the importance of fees, apps, ATM access, and overdraft protection, plus agreement on trust, app quality, and reasonableness of fees, while multi-select and top-3 items probe features used, switching triggers, and reasons for loyalty. Numeric free-text records typical balance and annual fees, and a full demographic block captures age, gender, race or ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, community type, and homeownership.
