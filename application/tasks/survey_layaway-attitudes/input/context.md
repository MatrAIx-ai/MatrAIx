# Layaway Attitudes

Familiarity with and attitudes toward layaway plans and how they compare with credit cards, Buy Now Pay Later, and saving up.

## About this survey

This survey gauges how familiar respondents are with layaway and whether they have used it, how often, for which purchases, and where it was offered. It positions layaway against other pay-over-time methods (Buy Now Pay Later, credit cards, store financing, personal loans, and saving up) and asks which people prefer. Attitudes are measured through agreement batteries on debt avoidance, being outdated, credit-card comparison, delayed possession, and displacement by Buy Now Pay Later, plus an appeal battery on layaway's key features such as no interest, no credit check, price lock, paying before pickup, and refunds. Additional items cover concerns, encouragements, best-fit situations, a top-3 pay-over-time priorities ranking, future intent, past satisfaction, a 0-10 recommendation scale, and an open comment field. It closes with a demographic block including age, gender, race or ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, community type, credit access, and financial situation.
