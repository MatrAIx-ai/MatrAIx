# Overdraft Experiences

How people experience checking account overdrafts and overdraft fees, the causes and burden of overdrafts, and the strategies they use to avoid them.

## About this survey

This survey screens for checking account ownership and where it is held, then captures overdraft frequency, fees paid, fee amounts, common causes, and enrollment in overdraft protection. It records the protections respondents use, how they learn of overdrafts, and recovery speed, and measures attitudes via agreement scales about fairness, understanding, usefulness, and switching, plus stress level. A frequency battery covers avoidance habits (checking balances, cushions, alerts, tracking bills, savings transfers), followed by fee-waiver requests, actions taken, financial burden, and a top-3 ranking of what would most help. Later sections cover fee trends, confidence, satisfaction, switching likelihood, and a likelihood-to-recommend scale, with numeric free-text for fees paid and typical balance. A comprehensive demographics block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, community type, month-to-month finances, pay frequency, and emergency savings.
