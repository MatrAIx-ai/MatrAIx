# Joint Household Finances

How households organize and manage their shared finances — accounts, bills, budgeting, and division of financial responsibilities.

## About this survey

This survey screens for whether respondents share money with another adult, how many earners the household has, and how finances are structured (fully combined to fully separate). It captures the shared accounts and tools used, how expenses are divided, and who handles bills, big purchases, and budgeting. Likert batteries assess fairness, clarity, ease, burden, and alignment, and a competence matrix rates how well the household handles bills, saving, budgeting, debt, and unexpected expenses. Further items cover active savings goals, money-management apps, spending transparency, private money, conflict resolution, and confidence in handling a large expense. Outcome questions include a top-three priorities ranking, share of income contributed, satisfaction, and likelihood of changing the money-management approach, with free-text items for number of earners and shared emergency savings. A full demographic block records age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, presence of children, region, urbanicity, and homeownership.
