# Nutrition Habits

Everyday eating patterns, food choices, and attitudes about nutrition.

## About this survey

This survey measures how people describe their overall eating habits, how many meals they eat, whether they follow a particular diet, and how often they eat breakfast, home-cooked meals, takeout, fast food, and snacks. It captures fruit and vegetable intake, foods people try to limit or eat more of, label-reading, sugary-drink and dessert frequency, supplement use, and weekly grocery spend. Attitudes are measured through agreement and importance batteries covering the value of healthy eating, time, affordability, confidence, and the drivers behind food choices, along with barriers, information sources, meal planning, and recent changes. It closes with satisfaction, intent to change, and a top-three improvements ranking. A comprehensive demographic block captures age band, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, who cooks, marital status, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural setting, and self-rated health.
