# Stock Investing Attitudes

People's beliefs, emotions, and behaviors around investing in the stock market.

## About this survey

This survey measures attitudes toward stock investing alongside actual behavior, starting with ownership, tenure, portfolio size, allocation, information sources, risk tolerance, and trading frequency. A large agreement Likert battery captures beliefs about wealth-building, gambling, market fairness, self-assessed knowledge, loss anxiety, market timing, and long-term versus active investing, while an importance battery rates the factors people weigh when evaluating a stock. Sub-topics include reactions to market drops, how often people check their portfolio, how fear affects participation, the assets they have traded, and confidence in the market's long-run prospects. It closes with satisfaction with returns, likelihood of investing more, a 0-to-10 recommendation score, a top-3 goals ranking, monthly investment amount, barriers, and an optional comment, followed by a full demographic block covering age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural setting, and investing experience.
