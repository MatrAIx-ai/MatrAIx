# Search Engine Preferences & Habits

Which search engines people rely on, how they search across devices, and how AI answer tools are reshaping their search habits.

## About this survey

This survey captures a person's primary and recently used search engines (Google, Bing, DuckDuckGo, Brave Search, Ecosia, and AI tools like Perplexity or ChatGPT), why they use them, search volume, and the devices and starting points they use. It measures how often they use features such as image search, maps, shopping, AI summaries, and voice search, then uses Likert agreement and importance batteries plus a matrix on feature frequency to gauge attitudes toward result quality, trust, tracking, ads, and AI answers. Later sections cover the shift from traditional search to AI chatbots, privacy steps, a top-3 switching-factor ranking, satisfaction, future intent, and a likelihood-to-recommend score, with free-text items on daily search volume and desired improvements. A full demographic block captures age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, relationship status, U.S. region, urban/rural setting, and self-rated technical skill.
