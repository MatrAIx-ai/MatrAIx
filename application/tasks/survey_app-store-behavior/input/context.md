# App Store Behavior

How people discover, evaluate, download, pay for, and remove mobile apps.

## About this survey

This survey studies app-store behavior: which store respondents use, how many apps they keep installed, how often they download, and how they discover new apps (charts, search, social media, ads, influencers, and word of mouth). It measures how they evaluate apps before installing, the role of ratings and written reviews, attitudes toward paying, annual app spending, and reactions to ads and in-app purchases, then uses Likert batteries on trust, privacy, and impulse plus an importance matrix on download-decision factors. Further sections cover permission-checking, review-writing, deletion triggers, a top-3 influences ranking, and outcome measures for satisfaction, confidence in judging apps, and willingness to try unknown developers. The demographic block captures age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urbanicity, mobile platform, and tech comfort.
