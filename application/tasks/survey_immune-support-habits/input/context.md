# Immune Support Habits

Everyday habits, foods, and products people use to support their general immune health.

## About this survey

This survey measures how much respondents focus on immune health and which wellness habits they follow (produce intake, sleep, exercise, hydration, stress management, hand hygiene, supplements, vaccination). It captures supplement use, types, and forms (vitamin C, D, zinc, multivitamin, probiotics, elderberry, herbals; pills, gummies, powders, drinks), immune-food frequency, seasonal variation, and information sources. An agreement-scale attitude battery covers belief in one's habits, supplements, diet versus supplements, difficulty judging claims, and sense of control, alongside trust in brand claims, professional consultation, monthly spend, the perceived most-important habit, a top-three habit ranking, overall importance, barriers, habit-tracker use, illness frequency, satisfaction, future intent, and open comments. Numeric free-text captures produce servings, monthly spend, and household size. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, employment, marital status, income, household size, region, urban or rural setting, and overall wellness interest. Content is framed attitudinally and kept consumer-oriented.
