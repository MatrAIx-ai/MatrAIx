# Email Client & Inbox Habits

Which email services and apps people use for personal mail and how they manage, secure, and feel about their inbox.

## About this survey

This survey captures a person's main email service (Gmail, Outlook, Yahoo, iCloud, Proton, and others) and the clients they read mail in, along with account count, what they use email for, checking frequency, and inbox state. It measures inbox-management tools, handling of promotions, unsubscribe and throwaway-address habits, notification settings, and email security practices such as two-factor authentication and phishing awareness, then uses Likert agreement and importance batteries plus a top-3 feature ranking to gauge attitudes toward overload, privacy, spam filtering, storage, and integration. Later items cover AI email features, storage pressure, paid upgrades, satisfaction, future intent, and a likelihood-to-recommend score, with free-text items on account count, daily volume, and desired improvements. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, relationship status, U.S. region, urban/rural setting, and self-rated technical skill.
