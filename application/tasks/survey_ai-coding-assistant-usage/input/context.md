# AI Coding Assistant Usage

How developers adopt, use, and evaluate AI coding assistants such as GitHub Copilot, Cursor, and Claude Code.

## About this survey

This survey examines developers' use of AI coding assistants, opening with a screener on frequency, tenure, and how the respondent writes code. It records the primary assistant and tools used and wanted, how the tools are accessed, the tasks and languages they are used for, suggestion-acceptance behavior, the share of AI-assisted code, spending, and preferred models. Attitudes are captured through an agreement Likert battery on productivity, trust, learning, bugs, and job concerns and a per-item importance matrix over assistant-selection criteria, plus a top-three ranking of benefits and a multi-select of concerns. Additional sections address answer accuracy, trust, employer policy, skill effects, autonomous agents, and satisfaction with quality and speed, followed by overall satisfaction, recommendation likelihood, usage-growth intent, switching intent, and overall stance. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering role, professional experience, employment, company size, industry, age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
