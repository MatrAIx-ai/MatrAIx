# Outdoor Exercise

Consumer habits, motivations, barriers, and satisfaction around exercising outdoors.

## About this survey

This survey measures how often and how long people have exercised outdoors, which activities they do (walking/hiking, running, cycling, water sports, team sports, outdoor classes, winter sports, yard work), where and when they go, how they get there, and whether they exercise alone or with others. It covers tracking-device and app use, session length and intensity, seasonal patterns, and motivations. Attitudes are captured through agreement, frequency, and importance Likert batteries covering mood benefits, consistency, weather and safety barriers, and what people value in an outdoor space, plus a "top 3" priorities ranking, overall satisfaction, a 0–10 recommendation scale, and future intent. Numeric free-text items capture recent days active, session and travel duration, and household size, followed by a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, marital status, household size, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural, and proximity to green space).
