# Prescription Drug Costs

What consumers pay for prescription medications and the strategies they use to manage those costs.

## About this survey

This survey screens for how many prescriptions respondents take, monthly out-of-pocket spending, and their drug coverage, then measures cost-management behaviors such as choosing generics, using discount apps like GoodRx, comparing pharmacy prices, mail-order and 90-day supplies, manufacturer coupons, and asking doctors about cheaper options. It probes cost- driven behaviors such as not filling or delaying a prescription and paying cash instead of using insurance, and includes agreement batteries on cost stress, price predictability, insurance clarity, and manufacturer pricing, plus an importance matrix on what matters when choosing where to fill a prescription. Later questions cover worry about future affordability, a top-three ranking of helpful changes, and closing satisfaction and word-of-mouth intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking, and numeric free-text items. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, marital status, region, community type, and primary insurance source.
