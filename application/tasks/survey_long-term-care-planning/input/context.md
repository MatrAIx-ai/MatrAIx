# Long-Term Care Planning

How adults think about, plan for, and expect to pay for long-term care (in-home care, assisted living, and nursing homes) as they age.

## About this survey

This survey opens with screening questions on whether respondents have thought about long-term care, their familiarity with its costs, and whether they have made plans or hold long-term care insurance. It then measures awareness of care arrangements, preferred care settings, and expected funding sources (savings, insurance, Medicare, Medicaid, home equity, and family). Likert batteries capture worries (cost, burden on family, loss of independence, care quality), attitudes about personal versus government responsibility, and the importance of factors such as aging in place, protecting assets, and provider quality, with a matrix rating planning priorities and a top-3 ranking of concerns. Additional items cover planning documents, advice sources, cost knowledge and savings amounts (free-text), likelihood to buy coverage, satisfaction with current preparation, and a 0–10 recommendation scale, plus an optional open comment. A comprehensive demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, education, employment, income, marital status, household size, homeownership, region, urbanicity, and whether the respondent has aging parents who may need care.
