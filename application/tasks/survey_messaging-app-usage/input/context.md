# Messaging App Usage

How people use messaging and chat apps to stay in touch with family, friends, and coworkers.

## About this survey

This survey studies messaging behavior: which apps respondents use (SMS, iMessage, WhatsApp, Messenger, Instagram, Snapchat, Telegram, Signal, Discord, WeChat, and others), their most-used app, daily message volume, and who they communicate with. It measures the purposes and features they rely on, read-receipt attitudes, reply expectations and pressure, and group-chat activity, then uses Likert batteries on connection, overwhelm, encryption, and cross-app friction plus an importance matrix on what matters in a messaging app. Additional sections cover voice/video calling, app switching, switch triggers, notification management, a top-3 values ranking, and outcome measures for satisfaction, likelihood to recommend, and future intent. The demographic block captures age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urbanicity, mobile platform, and tech comfort.
