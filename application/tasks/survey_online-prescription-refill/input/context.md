# Online Prescription Refill

Consumer use of, experience with, and attitudes toward refilling prescriptions online or through apps.

## About this survey

This survey measures whether people take regular medications and how often they refill prescriptions online, how long they have done so, and the channels they use (pharmacy app or website, patient portal, auto-refill, text links). It covers which pharmacies and services they use (CVS, Walgreens, Walmart, grocery pharmacies, Amazon Pharmacy, mail-order, independents), how they receive and pay for medications, feature use, and refill reminders. Attitudes are captured through agreement and satisfaction Likert batteries covering time savings, ease, trust, adherence, privacy, and specific service aspects, plus an importance battery, a "top 3" reasons ranking, problems experienced, likes and concerns, overall satisfaction, a 0–10 recommendation scale, and future intent. Numeric free-text items capture number of medications, days ahead requested, yearly refills, and household size, followed by a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, marital status, household size, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural, and insurance type).
