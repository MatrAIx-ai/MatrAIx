# Posture Awareness

Consumer awareness of posture and the habits, tools, and products used to maintain it.

## About this survey

This survey measures how often respondents think about their posture, how they rate it, and the situations where they notice poor posture (desk work, phone use, driving, standing). It captures daily sitting and phone-looking hours, posture-support habits and tools (ergonomic chairs, standing desks, stretch breaks, exercises, back supports, posture-reminder apps and wearables, braces), posture-friendly activities like yoga and pilates, and posture-related discomfort and its locations. An agreement-scale attitude battery covers effort, work and device effects, long-term importance, knowing what good posture feels like, and difficulty maintaining it, alongside movement-break frequency, information sources, professional advice, barriers, a top-three improvement ranking, motivation, overall importance, willingness to spend, satisfaction, improvement intent, and open comments. Numeric free-text captures phone-looking hours, movement breaks, and spend. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, employment, typical daily activity level, income, region, and urban or rural setting. Content is framed attitudinally and kept consumer-oriented.
