# Impulse Purchasing

How often people make unplanned purchases, what triggers them, and how impulse spending affects their finances and well-being.

## About this survey

This survey measures the frequency and monthly cost of impulse buying, the places it happens (physical stores, Amazon, social media, apps, warehouse clubs), the categories most often bought, the emotional and marketing triggers behind it, typical purchase size, and payment methods including buy-now-pay-later. Likert batteries assess impulsivity, emotional shopping, regret, deal-resistance, and sense of control; a frequency battery rates how specific situations lead to unplanned buys; and a "top 3" ranking captures what would most help curb spending alongside a checklist of strategies currently used. Additional questions cover returns, buyer's remorse, the influence of buy-now-pay-later, spending trends, interference with financial goals, satisfaction with self-control, and reduction intent, plus a seven-day count and an open reflection. It closes with a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urban/rural, and self-rated financial situation).
