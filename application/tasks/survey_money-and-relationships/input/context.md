# Money and Relationships

How money affects romantic relationships — communication, conflict, financial compatibility, and shared goals.

## About this survey

This survey begins with relationship status and length, then measures how couples talk about and manage money, which accounts they share, and how often and about what they argue. Likert batteries assess financial compatibility, money-related stress, trust, transparency, and comfort with income differences, and a tension matrix rates how much spending habits, income gaps, debt, family support, and long-term goals strain the relationship. Further items explore financial secrecy, knowledge of a partner's income and debt, the importance of early money talks, potential dealbreakers, decision-making on big purchases, and whether money has caused relationship trouble. Outcome questions cover a top-three ranking of valued financial traits in a partner, satisfaction with shared money management, willingness to seek help, and overall financial relationship health, with a free-text item for monthly money conversations. A full demographic block records age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, presence of children, region, urbanicity, and primary earner.
