# Internet Connectivity

How households experience their home internet service — the connection type, provider, speed, reliability, cost, and overall satisfaction.

## About this survey

This survey captures the type of home internet connection, provider, advertised speed, and primary uses, along with how many devices are connected, backup options, data caps, and how often the connection fails. It measures the disruption of outages, speed-test habits, switching history, and specific problems people encounter. Likert agreement and per-attribute satisfaction batteries rate speed sufficiency, reliability, value, provider choice, and satisfaction with speed, uptime, price, support, and Wi-Fi coverage, while a ranking item surfaces the top factors in choosing a provider. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert and satisfaction scales, split matrix batteries, a top-3 ranking, numeric and open free-text, plus satisfaction, recommendation, and switching-intent items, followed by a full demographic block covering age, gender, race/ethnicity, education, employment, work-from-home status, income, household, homeownership, region, and area type.
