# Trust in Health Websites and Sources

How much people trust different online health sources and what makes a health website seem credible.

## About this survey

This survey screens for health-website use and frequency, identifies which sites people have visited (Mayo Clinic, WebMD, Healthline, CDC/NIH, forums, social media, and others), and asks which single source they consider most trustworthy. A rated matrix battery scores trust across government agencies, hospital sites, commercial sites, patient forums, social media, and AI assistants, and multi-select questions capture the signals that raise or lower trust. Attitudinal Likert batteries measure ability to tell reliable sites apart, perceived prevalence of misinformation, worry about being misled, fear-based framing, and the influence of a doctor's recommendation. Later sections cover verification habits, whether unreliable information ever changed a decision, confidence in judging credibility, a top-3 ranking of trust factors, overall trust, and an optional open comment. It uses single-choice, multi-select, Likert and trust scales, a matrix battery, and numeric free-text, and closes with demographics covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, technology comfort, employment, relationship status, household size, region, urbanicity, and income.
