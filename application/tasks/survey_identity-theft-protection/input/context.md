# Identity Theft Protection

How people guard their personal identity, the tools and services they use, and their experiences with and responses to identity theft.

## About this survey

This survey screens for concern about identity theft and prior victimization, then details the types of identity theft experienced and the protection or credit-monitoring services used (LifeLock, Aura, IdentityForce, Experian, Credit Karma, and free tools), including monthly spend. It measures the protective steps taken (credit freezes, fraud alerts, two-factor authentication, password managers, document shredding, and alerts), password-management habits, and responses to data-breach notifications. Likert batteries capture sense of control, trust in companies, and perceived value of protection services; importance batteries rate desired service features; a worry battery covers specific risks; and a "top 3" ranking identifies which personal information respondents most want to protect. Additional questions address willingness to trade data for convenience, resolution time for past incidents, satisfaction, subscription intent, and a 0–10 recommendation scale. It closes with a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urban/rural, and technology comfort).
