# Annuity Consideration

Awareness of annuities and whether people would consider them as part of their retirement income.

## About this survey

This survey measures familiarity with annuities, current ownership, and understanding of annuity types (fixed, variable, indexed), along with the main goal a respondent would have for one. It captures the broader retirement income picture, concern about outliving savings, the appeal of guaranteed lifetime income, and likelihood of buying. Likert batteries assess attitudes about complexity, peace of mind, fees, liquidity, self-directed investing, and trust in insurers, and an importance matrix rates annuity features such as lifetime income, market protection, low fees, access to money, an heirs' benefit, and inflation protection. Further items cover top concerns, trusted advisors, comparison with market investing, and the perceived need for income beyond Social Security. Outcome questions include a top-three ranking of purchase motivators, overall favorability, and likelihood of researching further, with free-text items for the share of savings one would commit and the age an annuity makes sense. A full demographic block records retirement stage, age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, retirement savings, education, employment, marital status, household size, region, urbanicity, and risk tolerance.
