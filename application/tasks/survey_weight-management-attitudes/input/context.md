# Weight Management Attitudes

Consumer attitudes and everyday approaches toward managing weight.

## About this survey

This survey measures people's current weight goals, satisfaction, how often they think about weight, whether they track it, and the approaches they have used, along with their history and self-view of habits. Attitudes are captured through agreement, importance, and frequency batteries covering health value, confidence, conflicting advice, self-image, the roles of diet, activity, portions, sleep, stress, and support, and views on societal focus and size-inclusive approaches. It also covers motivations, barriers, information sources, trust in social-media advice, comfort discussing weight with a provider, intent to change, confidence in lasting change, a top-three needs ranking, and overall outlook. The content stays attitudinal and consumer-oriented. A comprehensive demographic block captures age band, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural setting, activity level, and self-rated health.
