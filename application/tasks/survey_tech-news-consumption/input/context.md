# Tech News Consumption

How people follow, read, evaluate, and share technology news across sites, newsletters, podcasts, video, and social media.

## About this survey

This survey measures how closely and how often respondents follow tech news, which sources and devices they use, which is primary, and which topics interest them most. It covers discovery habits, paid subscriptions and newsletters, behavior when big stories break, and attitudes toward hype, paywalls, misinformation, advertising, and AI-generated summaries. Likert agreement and source-selection importance batteries capture trust, overwhelm, and what people value in a source, while frequency items track headline-versus-article reading and sharing. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, split matrix batteries, a top-3 topic ranking, numeric and open free-text, plus satisfaction, recommendation, and future-intent items, followed by a comprehensive demographic block covering age, gender, race/ethnicity, education, employment, tech relationship, income, household, relationship status, region, and area type.
