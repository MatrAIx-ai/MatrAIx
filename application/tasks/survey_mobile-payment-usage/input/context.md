# Mobile Payment Usage

How consumers use mobile and contactless payments — the services they use, where and how often they pay, and their attitudes toward speed, security, and going walletless.

## About this survey

This survey screens for mobile payment use and tenure, then captures which services respondents use (Apple Pay, Google Pay, Samsung Pay, PayPal, store apps, contactless cards), their most-used option, frequency, locations, and linked funding sources. It measures decision drivers through an importance battery (speed, security, rewards, walletless convenience, tracking), attitudes via agreement scales, a top-3 ranking of reasons for use, and barriers to wider adoption. A frequency battery compares mobile tap, cash, and card use, followed by questions on cash displacement, past problems, fraud confidence, satisfaction, future intent, and a likelihood-to-recommend scale, with numeric free-text for weekly payment counts and share of purchases. A comprehensive demographics block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, community type, smartphone type, wearable ownership, and technology comfort.
