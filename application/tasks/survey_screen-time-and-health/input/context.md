# Screen Time and Health

Screen-time habits and their perceived effects on wellbeing.

## About this survey

This survey covers daily non-work and work screen hours, the devices responsible, how respondents judge their overall screen time, and dominant non-work activities. It measures attributed effects (eye strain, sleep trouble, neck/back discomfort, headaches, distraction, reduced activity), bedtime screen use, management steps, use of built-in digital-wellbeing tools and specific features, and break habits. It runs Likert batteries on agreement (sleep, eyes, over-use, connection, difficulty cutting back, household screen worry, benefit of breaks) and on how important managing each possible effect is, a top-3 concern ranking, concern level, cut-back attempts, impact on sleep and eye comfort, blue-light glasses use, satisfaction, reduction intent, and confidence in managing screen time, plus an open comment. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, a matrix battery split per item, a top-3 ranking, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured: age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urban/rural, screen-based work, children in the household, and self-rated health.
