# Multi-Device Usage

How people use, sync, and coordinate multiple computing devices across daily life.

## About this survey

This survey begins with a screener on how many devices the respondent uses, which ones, their primary device, and whether their devices share one ecosystem, then details the ecosystems they span, the continuity features they use (Handoff, universal clipboard, AirDrop, Nearby Share, Phone Link, browser sync), the tasks they carry across devices, and how they keep data in sync. Attitudes are measured with an agreement battery (seamlessness, sync reliability, hand-off frustration, lock-in, productivity, notification overload, and ecosystem simplicity), an importance matrix for multi-device priorities, and a top-three ranking of valued cross-device capabilities, plus questions on sync problems, frustrations, and ecosystem preference. Outcome items cover satisfaction, intent to add or consolidate devices, and likelihood to recommend an ecosystem, using single-choice, multi-select, Likert, matrix, ranking, and numeric free-text formats. The demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, relationship status, region, urban/suburban/rural setting, work situation, and overall tech comfort.
