# Inflation Attitudes

How people perceive inflation and how rising prices affect their households.

## About this survey

This survey covers how closely people follow inflation news, how much they feel prices have risen, their own estimate of the inflation rate, and where they notice the biggest increases across groceries, gas, housing, utilities, healthcare, and more. It measures how inflation has affected household finances, whether income is keeping pace, and the coping behaviors people adopt, from switching to cheaper brands to delaying purchases, cutting savings, or taking on extra work. Likert and worry batteries capture attitudes toward inflation as a national problem, sense of control, optimism, and concern about groceries, housing, saving, retirement, and debt, plus a ranked set of most-worrying spending categories. Additional items probe price expectations, protective steps such as high-yield savings, I bonds, or paying down debt, views on responsibility for inflation, and trust in official statistics. Closing items gauge stress, confidence, satisfaction, and intent, and a full demographic block records age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, marital status, household size, homeownership, region, and urbanicity.
