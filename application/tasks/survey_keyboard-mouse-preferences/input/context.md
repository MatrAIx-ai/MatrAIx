# Keyboard and Mouse Preferences

Preferences and habits around computer input devices — keyboard and mouse types, switches, layouts, brands, ergonomics, and satisfaction.

## About this survey

This survey explores how people choose and use keyboards, mice, and other pointing devices. It opens with daily typing hours and current device types, then covers connection methods, brands owned, mechanical switch and layout preferences, and primary computer uses. Importance batteries rate typing feel, ergonomics, wireless operation, customizability, price, and RGB/aesthetics, followed by a top-3 keyboard-qualities ranking. Agreement batteries probe productivity impact, willingness to pay a premium, typing discomfort, mechanical preference, and shortcut/macro customization, while further questions address ergonomic accessories, typing speed, cleaning habits, custom-keyboard experience, frustrations, satisfaction, purchase intent, and a 0–10 recommendation score. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, importance/agreement/satisfaction scales, and numeric and open free-text. Demographics include age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, occupation field, region, urban/rural, and self-rated gear enthusiasm.
