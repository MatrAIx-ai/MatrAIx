# Developer Community Participation

How developers engage with online and in-person technical communities, from lurking and asking questions to answering, contributing, and organizing.

## About this survey

This survey profiles participation across platforms such as Stack Overflow, GitHub, Reddit, Discord, blogging sites, and local meetups, capturing which is primary, how often people visit, and which activities they perform. It measures motivations, open-source contribution, reputation-building, and conference attendance, then uses Likert agreement batteries on belonging, newcomer friendliness, question anxiety, and career impact, plus frequency batteries on helpful answers, hostile responses, and useful contacts. It also probes how AI assistants are shifting where developers seek answers and how participation is trending. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, split matrix batteries, a top-3 ranking of motivations, numeric and open free-text, and satisfaction, recommendation, and future-intent items, followed by a full demographic block including role, coding experience, age, gender, race/ethnicity, education, employment, income, household, relationship status, region, and area type.
