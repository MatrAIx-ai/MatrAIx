# Home Care Services

Consumer experiences with and attitudes toward in-home care and helper services.

## About this survey

This survey covers whether a household currently uses in-home care, who the care is for, and which types of services are used or considered (personal care, homemaking, companionship, transportation, skilled nursing at home, overnight help). It measures how the service was found (agency, referral, independent hire, online platform, benefit program), weekly hours, how it is paid for, monthly spend, duration of use, caregiver consistency, most valuable tasks, and how care is scheduled. It runs Likert batteries on attitudes (caregiver training, trust, cost strain, communication, aging in place, difficulty finding a service) and on the importance of selection factors, plus a top-3 ranking of what matters most, biggest challenges, family oversight and stress, reliability, confidence in judging quality, how well needs are met, satisfaction, intent to continue, a 0–10 recommendation score, and an open comment. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, a matrix battery split per item, a top-3 ranking, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured: age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urban/rural, home ownership, and insurance status.
