# Credit Score Awareness

How aware people are of their credit score and how they monitor and manage it.

## About this survey

This survey measures whether respondents know their credit score and range, how often and where they check it — Credit Karma, Experian, bank tools, or AnnualCreditReport.com — and their familiarity with FICO versus VantageScore. It probes understanding of scoring factors, credit utilization, report errors and disputes, and concrete steps taken to improve credit, plus use of monitoring services, freezes, and fraud alerts. Likert batteries capture beliefs, worry, and perceived opacity, along with the importance of credit for mortgages, loan rates, renting, and card approval, while multi-select and top-3 items cover checking methods, score factors, real-world impacts, information sources, and reasons for caring. A 0–10 knowledge scale and numeric free-text record estimated score and number of open accounts, and a full demographic block captures age, gender, race or ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, community type, and homeownership.
