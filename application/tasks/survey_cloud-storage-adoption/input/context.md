# Cloud Storage Adoption & Usage

Which cloud storage services people adopt, what they store and share, and how they weigh cost, privacy, and reliability.

## About this survey

This survey captures a person's primary and secondary cloud storage services (Google Drive, iCloud, OneDrive, Dropbox, Amazon, and others), how long they've used them, how much data they keep, and what they store and why. It measures adoption path, devices, payment, access frequency, storage-limit pressure, photo backup, sharing features, and local-backup habits, then applies Likert agreement and importance batteries plus a top-3 selection-factor ranking to gauge attitudes toward accessibility, trust, privacy, lock-in, and dependence. Later items address privacy concern, data-loss experience, switching history, satisfaction, future intent, and a likelihood-to-recommend score, with free-text on years of use, monthly spend, and desired improvements. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, relationship status, U.S. region, urban/rural setting, and self-rated technical skill.
