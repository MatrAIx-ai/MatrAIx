# Childcare Cost Burden

How families experience the cost and financial burden of paying for childcare.

## About this survey

This survey covers whether and how much households pay for childcare, the types of care used (centers, home daycare, nannies, preschool, before/after-school and summer programs), weekly hours, monthly spending, and the share of income childcare consumes. It measures how families pay for care through FSAs, subsidies, tax credits, employer benefits, and family help, along with waitlists and the difficulty of finding care. Likert and frequency batteries capture the financial burden, trade-offs such as cutting work hours, delaying bills, dipping into savings, or taking on debt, plus a ranked set of factors families weigh when choosing care. Closing sections gauge financial stress, confidence, satisfaction with value, and future intent, and a full demographic block records age, gender, race and ethnicity, household income, education, employment, marital status, household size, region, urbanicity, and homeownership.
