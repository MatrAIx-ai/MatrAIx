# Laptop Purchasing

How consumers research, choose, and buy laptop computers — brands, budgets, purchase channels, decision criteria, and satisfaction with their choices.

## About this survey

This survey maps the laptop buying journey from research to purchase to satisfaction. It screens for ownership and recency, then captures usage, current brand and operating system, spend, budget range, where people shop, and how they research (reviews, YouTube, forums, in-store, AI chatbots). Importance batteries rate purchase criteria such as price, battery life, performance, display, portability, brand, and build quality, followed by a top-3 feature ranking and preferences for screen size, RAM, and form factor. Agreement batteries probe brand willingness-to-pay, upgrade cadence, spec-comparison difficulty, warranties, and repairability, while further questions cover new-vs-refurbished, sales timing, payment method, replacement triggers, satisfaction, brand loyalty, and a 0–10 recommendation score. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, importance/agreement/satisfaction scales, and numeric and open free-text. Demographics include age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, region, urban/rural, and self-rated tech savviness.
