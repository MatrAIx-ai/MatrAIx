# Remote Patient Monitoring

Consumer experience with provider-run remote patient monitoring programs.

## About this survey

This survey covers whether respondents have participated in a remote monitoring program, their current status and its focus, how they learned about it, enrollment length, what data is collected (blood pressure, weight, glucose, heart rate, oxygen, activity, symptoms), how and how often readings are submitted, who supplied the equipment and its monthly cost, the devices involved, and how the care team contacts them. It measures care-team responsiveness and effect on in-person visits, then runs Likert batteries on agreement (ease, time saved, connection, early detection, worth the effort, privacy, burden) and on the importance of program factors, a top-3 benefit ranking, confidence, concerns, technical problems, comfort sharing data, satisfaction, a 0–10 recommendation score, and continuation and expansion intent, plus an open comment. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, a matrix battery split per item, a top-3 ranking, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured: age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urban/rural, insurance status, household condition management, and general comfort with health technology.
