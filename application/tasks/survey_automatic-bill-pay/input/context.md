# Automatic Bill Pay

How households use automatic bill payment to manage recurring bills, and their experiences, trust, and concerns with autopay.

## About this survey

This survey screens whether and how long respondents have used autopay, how many bills are automated, which bills they automate, how they set it up, and which funding source they use. It captures the main reason for using autopay, review habits, and problems experienced such as overdrafts, forgotten subscriptions, incorrect charges, and cancellation difficulties. Attitudes are measured through agreement batteries on convenience, spending visibility, low-balance worry, biller trust, and preference for manual approval, plus an importance battery on avoiding late fees, saving time, protecting credit, earning rewards, and enrollment discounts. Additional items cover the biggest concern, bills deliberately kept off autopay, use of reminders, reasons for canceling, a top-3 enrollment-drivers ranking, satisfaction, expansion intent, and a 0-10 recommendation scale, plus open numeric and text fields. It closes with a demographic block including age, gender, race or ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, community type, and comfort with digital money tools.
