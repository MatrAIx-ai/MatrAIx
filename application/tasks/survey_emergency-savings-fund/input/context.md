# Emergency Savings Fund

How people build, hold, and use a dedicated emergency or rainy-day savings fund.

## About this survey

This survey examines whether respondents have emergency savings, how many months of expenses they could cover, and how they would handle a sudden $400 expense. It captures where the money is kept — high-yield accounts like Ally, Marcus, and SoFi, traditional savings, cash, or investments — along with target fund size, contribution habits, and past withdrawals and their causes. Likert batteries measure attitudes, importance of liquidity and returns, preparedness, and financial stress, while multi-select and top-3 questions cover barriers, strategies, and reasons for saving; numeric free-text items record current balance, monthly contributions, and take-home income. A full demographic block records age, gender, race or ethnicity, income, education, employment, income stability, household size, marital status, region, community type, and homeownership.
