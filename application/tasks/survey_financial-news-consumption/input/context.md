# Financial News Consumption

How people follow financial and economic news and how it informs their money decisions.

## About this survey

This survey measures how often and how closely people follow financial news, the formats and named outlets they rely on (from CNBC and the Wall Street Journal to Reddit, YouTube, and TikTok), and which topics they track most. It captures behaviors such as paid subscriptions, reactions to major market news, and money decisions made in response to coverage. Likert batteries assess attitudes about whether news improves decisions, causes anxiety, feels hyped, or is easy to understand, and a trust matrix rates credibility across established outlets, TV, social media, podcasts, bank content, and personal networks. Additional items cover timing and frequency of consumption, tracking tools, fact-checking habits, a top-three ranking of what people want from financial news, changes over time, likelihood to recommend, and overall satisfaction, with free-text items for daily minutes and number of sources followed. A full demographic block records age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urbanicity, investing status, and general interest in finance.
