# Two-Factor Authentication

How people adopt and use two-factor and multi-factor authentication to protect their online accounts.

## About this survey

This survey examines whether and how respondents use two-factor authentication, covering adoption timing, triggers, and the specific methods they rely on (SMS codes, authenticator apps, push prompts, hardware keys, biometrics, backup codes, and passkeys). It measures which account types are protected, backup-code practices, and hassle frequency, then uses Likert batteries on security value and convenience plus a matrix rating the perceived security of each 2FA method. Additional sections cover lockouts, hardware keys, passkey adoption, barriers to wider use, workplace requirements, a top-3 values ranking, and outcome measures for satisfaction, likelihood to recommend, and future intent. The demographic block captures age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urbanicity, tech comfort, and relationship to tech work.
