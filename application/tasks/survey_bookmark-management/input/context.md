# Bookmark Management

How people save, organize, and revisit bookmarks and read-later content.

## About this survey

This survey opens with a screener on how the respondent saves content to revisit, how many bookmarks they have, how organized they are, and how often they return to saved items, then details the saving methods and tools used (browser bookmarks, reading lists, Pocket, Raindrop, Instapaper, Readwise Reader, Notion, and others), their organizing approach, what they bookmark, and whether bookmarks sync. Attitudes are measured with an agreement battery (organization, save-and-forget, tab overload, productivity, losing track, read-later apps, and backlog guilt), a first-place-to-look item, an importance matrix for bookmarking-tool features, and a top-three ranking of reasons to save, plus frustrations and tab-management habits. Outcome items cover satisfaction, findability confidence, tool-switching intent, and likelihood to recommend a method, using single-choice, multi-select, Likert, matrix, ranking, and numeric free-text formats. The demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, relationship status, region, urban/suburban/rural setting, primary browser, and overall tech comfort.
