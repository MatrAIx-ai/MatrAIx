# Health Savings & Flexible Spending Account Usage

How consumers set up, fund, and spend tax-advantaged health accounts such as HSAs and FSAs.

## About this survey

This survey opens by screening whether respondents hold an HSA, FSA, dependent care FSA, limited purpose FSA, or HRA, then explores how the account was offered, who administers it, how much they and their employer contribute, and what they spend the funds on. It measures payment and tracking behaviors (debit card use, reimbursement claims, investing an HSA balance), an agreement battery about understanding rules, tax value, app usability, and fear of forfeiting funds, and importance ratings for the factors driving contribution decisions. Later sections drill into use-it-or-lose-it deadlines, forfeited money, help- seeking, and a top-three ranking of desired improvements, followed by satisfaction, likelihood to recommend, and intent to keep contributing. Question types include single- choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking, and numeric free-text items. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, marital status, region, community type, and primary insurance source.
