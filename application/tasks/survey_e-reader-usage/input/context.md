# E-Reader Usage

Digital reading habits and e-reader ownership — devices and brands used, where e-books come from, features valued, and attitudes toward digital versus print reading.

## About this survey

This survey explores how people read e-books and use e-readers. After screeners on e-book reading and device ownership, it captures reading tenure, devices used, e-reader brand, where books are obtained, formats read, annual book count, digital-vs-print share, reading frequency, genres, subscriptions, and monthly spend. Importance batteries rate screen quality, battery life, price, adjustable light, waterproofing, and size, plus a top-3 e-reader-features ranking. Agreement batteries probe convenience, print preference, eye comfort, cost savings, reading more, and e-book ownership concerns, while further questions cover features used, where reading happens, likes and dislikes, satisfaction, purchase intent, and a 0–10 recommendation score. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, importance/agreement/frequency/satisfaction scales, and numeric and open free-text. Demographics include age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, region, urban/rural, and self-described reader type.
