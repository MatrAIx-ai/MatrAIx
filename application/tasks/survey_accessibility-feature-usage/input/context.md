# Accessibility Feature Usage

How people use built-in accessibility features across their devices and apps, and how well those features meet their needs and preferences.

## About this survey

This survey profiles use of accessibility features such as larger text, dark mode, captions, screen readers, magnification, voice control, color filters, and reduce-motion, capturing which features people use, on which devices, which they rely on most, and why. It covers how features were discovered, caption and voice-control frequency, which platform is seen as most accessible, and encounters with apps that work poorly. Likert agreement and per-context satisfaction batteries gauge ease of finding settings, broad usefulness, and satisfaction across phones, computers, websites, and streaming, while a ranking item surfaces the most valuable features. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert and satisfaction scales, split matrix batteries, a top-3 ranking, numeric and open free-text, plus satisfaction, recommendation, and future-use items, followed by a full demographic block covering age, gender, race/ethnicity, education, employment, disability status, income, household, relationship status, region, and area type.
