# Doctor Communication

How patients experience and evaluate communication with their doctors and health providers.

## About this survey

This survey measures patients' experiences communicating with their doctors and primary care providers. It begins with screener questions on whether people have a regular provider, visit frequency, and communication channels used, then explores appointment behaviors such as preparing questions, feeling rushed, and following up when confused. A matrix battery rates how often the doctor listens, explains clearly, involves the patient in decisions, and shows respect, while additional Likert items capture comfort, trust, jargon, and clarity of next steps. A top-3 ranking identifies the communication qualities patients value most, and further questions address portal messaging, response times, honesty, cultural and language concordance, satisfaction, likelihood to recommend, and retention. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, community type, primary language, and insurance status. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several rating scales, a matrix battery, a ranking item, and numeric free-text.
