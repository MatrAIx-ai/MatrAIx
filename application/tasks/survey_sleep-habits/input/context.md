# Sleep Habits

Everyday sleep habits, routines, and attitudes among adults.

## About this survey

This survey measures how much people sleep on weeknights and weekends, their bedtimes and wake times, sleep quality, how long they take to fall asleep, night wakings, and schedule consistency. It captures wind-down routines, common disruptors, screen use in bed, caffeine timing, napping, and the strategies people try to sleep better. Attitudes and impacts are measured through agreement, frequency, and importance batteries covering sleep priority, stress, daytime mood, energy, focus and appetite effects, and the elements of a good sleep environment. It closes with sleep-aid use, satisfaction, intent to change, and a top-three improvements ranking. A comprehensive demographic block captures age band, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, shift work, household size, marital status, bed-sharing, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural setting, and self-rated health.
