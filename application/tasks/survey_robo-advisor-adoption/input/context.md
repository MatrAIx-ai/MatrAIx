# Robo-Advisor Adoption

How investors adopt, use, and trust automated algorithm-based investing services.

## About this survey

This survey explores robo-advisor adoption from awareness and platform choice (Betterment, Wealthfront, Schwab Intelligent Portfolios, Vanguard, Fidelity Go, SoFi, Acorns) through how long people have used one, account types, amounts invested, monthly contributions, funding method, and features used. It measures how users discovered the service, how they invested before, and how often they check in, then uses agreement and importance Likert batteries to gauge trust in algorithms, perceived simplicity and value, and comparison to human advisors. Sub-topics include behavior during market downturns, perceived drawbacks, investing confidence, and likelihood of continued use, followed by satisfaction, a 0-to-10 recommendation score, a top-3 reasons ranking, and an optional comment. A comprehensive demographic block records age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural setting, and self-rated investing experience.
