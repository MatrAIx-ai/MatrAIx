# Adult Vaccination Attitudes

Adults' attitudes toward and habits around routine vaccinations, including the annual flu shot and other recommended adult immunizations.

## About this survey

This survey measures respondents' overall stance on adult vaccination, which vaccines they have received, where they get vaccinated, and how they decide. It includes Likert batteries on beliefs about vaccine value, safety, and convenience, and a matrix rating how much different factors (cost, convenience, doctor's recommendation, illness concern, protecting family) influence the decision, plus reasons for hesitation framed attitudinally. It also covers record-keeping, walk-in pharmacy use, likelihood of getting future recommended vaccines, satisfaction with access, a likelihood-to-encourage-others scale, and a top-3 ranking of what would encourage staying up to date. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, a matrix battery split per item, numeric free-text items, and a demographic block covering age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, area type, insurance, children in household, and self-rated health.
