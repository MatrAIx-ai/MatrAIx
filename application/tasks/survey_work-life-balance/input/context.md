# Work–Life Balance

How working adults manage the boundary between their job and personal life and how that balance affects their everyday well-being.

## About this survey

This survey opens with a screener on employment status, weekly hours, work arrangement, tenure, and commute, then measures after-hours connectivity, paid-time-off usage, and the specific tactics people use to protect personal time. Attitudinal Likert batteries capture ability to disconnect, manager respect for personal time, guilt around time off, and a rated battery on how work stress affects sleep, mood, relationships, exercise, and eating. Further sections cover schedule flexibility, remote-work effects, caregiving strain, burnout frequency, satisfaction, and intent to switch jobs, including a top-3 ranking of the benefits that would help most and a 0–10 recommendation score. It uses single-choice, multi-select, Likert and importance scales, a matrix battery, numeric free-text, and an optional open comment, and closes with a comprehensive demographic block covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, occupation type, relationship status, household size, region, urbanicity, and income.
