# Ergonomics at Work

Workplace ergonomics: workspace setup, comfort, and employer support.

## About this survey

This survey covers where respondents work, daily seated hours, the nature of their work, and which ergonomic equipment they use (adjustable chair, sit-stand desk, external peripherals, monitor/laptop stands, footrest, supports). It measures workspace comfort, types of discomfort experienced, employer-provided support, whether they've had a formal assessment, personal spend on equipment, break habits, brands used (Herman Miller, Steelcase, Autonomous, Uplift, IKEA), and standing-desk use. It runs Likert batteries on agreement (comfort, employer care, productivity, know-how, discomfort's impact, breaks, value of investing) and on how important each ergonomic feature is, a top-3 desired- improvement ranking, awareness of good practices, barriers, discomfort's effect on productivity, views on responsibility, satisfaction, investment intent, and confidence in setting up a workspace, plus an open comment. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, a matrix battery split per item, a top-3 ranking, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured: age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urban/rural, work arrangement, and field of work.
