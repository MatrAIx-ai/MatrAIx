# Fitness Tracker Adoption

Consumer adoption and everyday use of wearable fitness trackers and smartwatches.

## About this survey

This survey measures whether respondents own a fitness tracker or smartwatch, the device type and brand (Apple, Fitbit, Garmin, Oura, Whoop, and others), how long they have owned it, what it cost, and what motivated adoption. It captures wearing habits, the metrics people follow, how often they check the companion app, subscription use, and how central the device is to their routine. Attitudes are measured through agreement, satisfaction, and importance batteries covering activity change, data trust, reward, data anxiety, privacy, battery, accuracy, comfort, app quality, and value, plus goal-setting, data-sharing with providers, non-fitness feature use, frustrations, churn reasons, retention and upgrade intent, a likelihood-to-recommend score, and a top-three improvements ranking. A comprehensive demographic block captures age band, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural setting, technology comfort, and self-rated health.
