# No-Code & Low-Code Tool Adoption

How individuals and teams adopt no-code and low-code tools to build software without traditional programming.

## About this survey

This survey studies adoption of no-code and low-code tools, opening with a screener on usage, tenure, and the respondent's technical background. It records what people build, the specific tools, automation platforms, and website builders they use, whether usage is personal or business, who in the organization uses these tools, how they were discovered, and spending. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on capability, time savings, limits, and lock-in and a per-item importance matrix over tool-selection criteria, plus a top-three ranking of reasons for adoption and a multi-select of limitations. Additional sections address how often users hit limits, combining no-code with custom code, AI features, and satisfaction with ease of use and flexibility, followed by overall satisfaction, recommendation likelihood, and future-use intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey ends with a demographic block covering occupation, employment, company size, industry, age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, U.S. region, and urbanicity, alongside the technical-background item captured early on.
