# Monitor Setup

Computer monitor and display setups — number and specs of screens, brands, mounting, purchase criteria, eye-strain habits, and satisfaction.

## About this survey

This survey profiles the display setups people use for work and personal computing. It captures the number of monitors, total displays, primary uses, and detailed specs of the main monitor (size, resolution, refresh rate, panel type), plus brand, form factor, mounting, connections, and spend. Importance batteries rate resolution, refresh rate, color accuracy, size, price, and adjustability, followed by a top-3 monitor-qualities ranking. Agreement batteries probe multi-monitor productivity, refresh-rate benefit, eye strain, willingness to pay, and whether the setup meets needs, while further questions cover eye-strain-reduction habits, dark mode, color calibration, frustrations, satisfaction, upgrade intent, and a 0–10 recommendation score. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, importance/agreement/frequency/satisfaction scales, and numeric and open free-text. Demographics include age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, work arrangement, region, urban/rural, and self-rated display-tech interest.
