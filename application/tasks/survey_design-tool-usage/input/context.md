# Design & Creative Tool Usage

Which graphic, UX, and creative design tools people use, how they use them, and how satisfied they are.

## About this survey

This survey profiles the respondent's design role and experience, then maps the types of design work they do and the tools they use (Figma, Adobe Photoshop/Illustrator/XD/InDesign, Canva, Sketch, Affinity, Procreate, GIMP, Inkscape, Framer, and others), including their primary tool, hardware, stylus use, and usage frequency. It measures how they learned, how they pay and spend, workflow accelerators, use of AI-assisted design features, and collaboration and handoff methods, followed by Likert agreement and satisfaction batteries and an importance battery on tool-selection factors. It closes with a top-3 improvement ranking, attitudes toward AI-generated art, switching behavior and drivers, how they follow trends, a likelihood-to-recommend score, overall satisfaction, switch intent, and an open item. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, a satisfaction matrix split per item, a ranking, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured include age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, role/field, self-rated skill, household size, relationship status, U.S. region, and urban/suburban/rural.
