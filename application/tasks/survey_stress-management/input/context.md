# Stress Management

Everyday stress and the ways consumers manage it.

## About this survey

This survey measures self-reported everyday stress levels and frequency, a self-rating of how well people manage stress, and the main sources and timing of their stress. It captures the activities and one top approach people use to relax, how often they make time to unwind, the apps and tools they use, and minutes spent on relaxation. Attitudes and impacts are measured through agreement, frequency, and importance batteries covering coping effectiveness, manageability, self-priority, social support, and the value of exercise, sleep, support, hobbies, and mindfulness, plus effects on sleep, energy, focus, eating, and relationships. It also covers barriers, mindfulness frequency, workplace resources, comfort seeking professional help, year-over-year change, satisfaction, intent, and a top-three helpers ranking. The content stays attitudinal and consumer-oriented. A comprehensive demographic block captures age band, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, marital status, children in the household, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural setting, and self-rated health.
