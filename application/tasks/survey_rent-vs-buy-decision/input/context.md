# Rent vs. Buy Decision

How people weigh renting versus buying a home — the factors, attitudes, barriers, and intentions behind the decision.

## About this survey

This survey captures the respondent's current housing situation, tenure, monthly housing cost, and cost burden, then asks which way they would lean today and the specific factors pulling them toward renting or buying. It probes barriers to purchasing, down-payment savings and expected down-payment percentage. Likert batteries measure beliefs about homeownership as an investment, the "American Dream," whether renting is wasteful, feeling priced out, and confidence in understanding ownership costs; an importance battery rates decision criteria such as affordability, equity, flexibility, maintenance, schools, and space; and a "top 3" ranking identifies the most decisive factors. Additional questions cover local affordability, the impact of rising prices and rates, information sources, expected tenure, decision stress, confidence, satisfaction, and likelihood of buying within three years. It closes with a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, children, region, and urban/rural).
