# Printer Usage

Home and personal printer ownership and use — printer types and brands, what people print, ink and toner costs, reliability, and satisfaction.

## About this survey

This survey examines how consumers own and use printers at home. After an access screener and ownership tenure, it captures printer type and brand, primary printing tasks, monthly print volume, frequency, connection methods, color-vs-mono balance, and ink/toner buying habits and annual spend. Importance batteries rate purchase price, ink cost, print quality, wireless printing, scan/copy features, and speed, plus a top-3 factors ranking. Agreement batteries probe ink-cost frustration, reliability, declining print use, wireless-setup ease, and brand lock-in, while further questions cover printer problems, replenishment subscriptions, where supplies are bought, alternatives when no printer is available, paperless consideration, satisfaction, purchase intent, and a 0–10 recommendation score. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, importance/agreement/frequency/satisfaction scales, and numeric and open free-text. Demographics include age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, children in home, remote-work status, region, and urban/rural.
