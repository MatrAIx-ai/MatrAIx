# Over-the-Counter Medicine Usage

How consumers buy, choose, and use over-the-counter (non-prescription) medicines for everyday needs.

## About this survey

This survey screens for how often respondents use OTC products and which categories they keep at home, then measures behaviors such as where they shop, brand versus store-brand choices, monthly spending, what they rely on when choosing, and how carefully they read labels and active ingredients. It covers the reasons OTC products are used, the role they play before seeing a doctor, and includes agreement batteries on convenience, store-brand effectiveness, confidence, label clarity, and dosing care. An importance matrix rates price, brand, speed, pharmacist input, and side effects, followed by medicine-cabinet stocking, when a symptom warrants a doctor, a top-three ranking of purchase priorities, and closing satisfaction and likelihood to recommend. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking, and numeric free-text items. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, marital status, presence of children, region, community type, and primary insurance source.
