# Pharmacy Experience

Consumers' experiences with their chosen pharmacy, pharmacists, and pharmacy services.

## About this survey

This survey screens for how often respondents use a pharmacy and which one they use most, then measures behaviors such as pickup and refill methods, app usage, wait times, and use of added services like vaccinations, consultations, and prescription synchronization. It probes interactions with the pharmacist, problems experienced (out-of-stock, coverage issues, delays), and includes agreement batteries on convenience, staff helpfulness, accuracy, and trust, along with quality ratings for accuracy, speed, friendliness, and the app. An importance matrix covers what matters when choosing a pharmacy, followed by switching behavior, loyalty programs, a top-three ranking of priorities, and closing satisfaction and likelihood to recommend. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking, and numeric free-text items. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, marital status, region, community type, and primary insurance source.
