# Video Editing Software

Which video editing software people use, how they edit, and how satisfied they are with their tools.

## About this survey

This survey profiles the respondent's editing role and experience, then maps the kinds of video they produce and the tools they use (Adobe Premiere Pro, DaVinci Resolve, Final Cut Pro, CapCut, iMovie, After Effects, Filmora, Vegas Pro, Descript, and open-source editors), including their primary tool, device, editing frequency, hours, resolution, and the editing tasks they perform. It measures how they learned, payment and spend, use of AI-assisted features, and editing hardware, followed by Likert agreement and satisfaction batteries and an importance battery on tool-selection factors. It closes with a top-3 improvement ranking, frustrations, switching behavior, publishing destinations, a likelihood-to-recommend score, overall satisfaction, switch intent, and an open item. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, a satisfaction matrix split per item, a ranking, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured include age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, professional relationship to editing, self-rated skill, household size, relationship status, U.S. region, and urban/suburban/rural.
