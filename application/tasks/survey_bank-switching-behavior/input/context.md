# Bank Switching Behavior

Whether, why, and how often people switch their primary bank, and what keeps them loyal or drives them to leave.

## About this survey

This survey screens for a primary checking relationship, the type of institution used, tenure, and recent switching or consideration, with free-text counts of banks used and lifetime switches. It captures triggers for switching (fees, service, rates, bonuses, relocation, app quality) and the features that matter most in a bank. Likert batteries measure the importance of factors that retain customers (fees, rates, app, branch access, service) and attitudes about switching hassle, loyalty, rate-chasing, bonuses, and direct-deposit friction, plus a top-3 ranking of switch triggers and a multi-select on barriers to switching. Additional items cover bonus-chasing behavior, research methods, fee experiences and annual fee spend (free-text), the ease of past switches, keeping old accounts, loyalty, and whether switching feels worth the effort, ending with a 0–10 recommendation score and an optional comment. A full demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, education, employment, income, marital status, household size, region, urbanicity, and comfort with online banking.
