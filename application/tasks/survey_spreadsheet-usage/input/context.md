# Spreadsheet Usage & Skills

Which spreadsheet tools people use, what they use them for, and how confident and skilled they are with formulas, pivots, and automation.

## About this survey

This survey captures a person's primary and recently used spreadsheet tools (Excel, Google Sheets, Numbers, LibreOffice Calc, Airtable, and others), how long and how much they use them, and their main purposes and context. It measures self-rated skill, which features and functions they use, pivot-table and macro experience, learning methods, and collaboration, then applies Likert agreement, a self-confidence matrix across specific skills, and an importance battery to gauge attitudes toward reliance, confidence, error worry, and tool priorities. Later items cover AI spreadsheet help, a top-3 skills-to-improve ranking, help-seeking behavior, satisfaction, and a likelihood-to-recommend score, with free-text on experience length, weekly hours, and most useful uses. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, role or field, household size, relationship status, U.S. region, urban/rural setting, and self-rated technical skill.
