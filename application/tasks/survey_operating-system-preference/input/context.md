# Operating System Preferences & Usage

How people choose, use, and feel about the desktop and laptop operating systems on their computers.

## About this survey

This survey captures which operating systems (Windows, macOS, Linux distributions, ChromeOS) a person uses on personal and work machines, how they came to use them, and the factors driving their choice such as compatibility, gaming, security, control, and ecosystem. It measures customization and command-line habits, update behavior, switching history, and multi-OS use, then uses Likert batteries and matrix items to gauge agreement, satisfaction, feature importance, and how often problems occur. Closing sections cover installation habits, privacy concerns, satisfaction, future intent, and a likelihood-to-recommend score, alongside free-text items on daily usage and desired changes. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, relationship status, U.S. region, urban/rural setting, occupation, and self-rated technical skill.
