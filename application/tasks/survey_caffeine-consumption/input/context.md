# Caffeine Consumption

Everyday caffeine consumption habits, choices, and attitudes.

## About this survey

This survey covers how often respondents consume caffeine, which products (coffee, tea, energy drinks, soda, supplements, chocolate), how long they have been regular consumers, daily quantity, timing of the first drink, reasons for use, where they get their drinks, their favorite type, coffee sources (home, Starbucks, Dunkin', local cafés, convenience stores), weekly spend, most-consumed product, and decaf use. It runs Likert batteries on agreement (reliance, taste and ritual, focus, worry, sleep effects, ability to cut back, social role) and on the importance of factors when choosing a drink, a top-3 reasons ranking, reliance, late-day cutoff, noticed effects, cut-back attempts, attention to caffeine content, satisfaction, a 0–10 recommendation score, and intent to change, plus an open comment. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, a matrix battery split per item, a top-3 ranking, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured: age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urban/rural, self-rated health, and sleep quality.
