# Dividend Investing

How individuals approach investing in dividend-paying stocks and funds, and the role dividend income plays in their broader financial strategy.

## About this survey

This survey opens with screener and usage questions about whether respondents own dividend investments, how long they have held them, which accounts and funds they use, and how they handle dividends received. It then measures evaluation criteria through an importance battery (yield, dividend growth, payout ratio, financial health, total return, taxes), attitudes via agreement scales, target yields, reactions to dividend cuts, research sources, and reliance on dividend income. Later sections cover satisfaction, future intent, and a likelihood-to-recommend scale, with a top-3 ranking of investing goals and numeric free-text items for annual dividend income and number of holdings. A comprehensive demographics block captures age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, community type, investable assets, and risk tolerance.
