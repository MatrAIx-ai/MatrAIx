# Financial Literacy Self-Assessment

How people rate their own financial knowledge, where they learned it, and how they try to improve it.

## About this survey

This survey captures respondents' self-rated financial knowledge overall and relative to peers, who manages the household money, and where they learned and currently get financial information. A confidence matrix rates understanding across budgeting, debt and credit, saving, investing, retirement, taxes, insurance, and mortgages, and a short set of objective knowledge questions covers compound interest, inflation, diversification, bond prices, mortgage terms, and credit-card interest. Behavioral items track budgeting, spending tracking, actions taken, and tools used. Attitudes are measured with agreement and frequency batteries, and a top-3 ranking identifies the topics respondents most want to learn. It closes with satisfaction, importance, future learning intent, a 0-10 confidence score, and an open comment. The comprehensive demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, area type, and whether they took a personal-finance class, so personas can be matched.
