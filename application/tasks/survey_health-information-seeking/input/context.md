# Health Information Seeking

How people look for health information, which sources and devices they use, and how they act on what they find.

## About this survey

This survey measures how often respondents seek health information, where they look first, and the full set of sources and devices they use, including AI chatbots and symptom searches. It captures the topics researched most, how people judge trustworthiness, and confidence in finding reliable information. Attitudinal Likert batteries cover effort, understandability, feeling overwhelmed, conflicting recommendations, and sense of control, and a rated matrix battery scores trust across doctors, government agencies, general websites, social media, and AI assistants. Later sections examine discussing findings with a doctor, reconciling conflicting advice, searching on behalf of others, actions taken as a result, health-related anxiety, a top-3 ranking of source qualities, overall satisfaction, and an optional open comment. It uses single-choice, multi-select, Likert and trust scales, a matrix battery, and numeric free-text, and closes with demographics covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, technology comfort, employment, household size, region, urbanicity, and income.
