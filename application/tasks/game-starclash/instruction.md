# Starclash

You are a crew member aboard a starship, sharing one open room with the rest of the crew. There is no map of separate rooms — everyone moves freely around the same continuous space, and you can only see, talk to, or challenge people who are physically near you. If someone you want to interact with is far away, walk toward them first.

You hold a hand of cards — Rock, Paper, and Scissors — and a number of stars that represent your standing. Rock beats Scissors, Scissors beats Paper, Paper beats Rock. When you challenge someone (or someone challenges you and you accept), you each secretly play one card from your hand; whoever wins takes a star from the loser. If your stars run out, you are out of the game.

Each crew member starts with 3 stars and 4 cards. The intended end state of the round is that everyone has used all of their cards, so a strong run is one where you play through your hand while preserving your standing. You are not required to collaborate, and there is no single correct strategy: you may compete, cooperate, bluff, or mix approaches. For example, a tie can be a sensible way to preserve survival without forcing a win, but you should choose whatever feels natural for your persona.

At any moment you can:

- **Move** to a new position in the room, to get closer to someone or to put distance between yourself and someone else.
- **Talk publicly** to anyone nearby, so everyone near you can hear it.
- **Send a private message** to someone nearby, seen only by the two of you.
- **Challenge** someone nearby to a Rock-Paper-Scissors duel for stars.
- **Accept or decline** a challenge someone else sends you.
- **Wait**, if you'd rather hold back and watch for now.

**Play the game in your own style.** There is no single correct strategy here, and no "right" way to spend your shift. Some crew members will want to size everyone up before making a move; others will dive into duels right away; others may prefer to talk their way through the room, form alliances, or bluff. Act as your persona naturally would — your goal is simply to play the game the way you genuinely would, given who you are.

Your actions and any reasoning you give for them will be recorded as part of the game log, alongside the outcome of every duel and the final standings at the end of the shift. There is no separate report to fill out — simply play, moment by moment, in character.
