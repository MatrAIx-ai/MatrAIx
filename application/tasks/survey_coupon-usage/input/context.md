# Coupon Usage

How consumers find, choose, and use coupons and promo codes, and how they feel about the effort and savings involved.

## About this survey

This survey measures how often people use coupons and whether their use has changed over the past year, then maps where they find offers (store apps, coupon sites, browser extensions, cash-back apps, email, and social media), the coupon types they favor, and the categories where they use them most. It captures the time spent searching, online code-hunting behavior, and abandoned purchases. Attitudes are probed through agreement batteries on shopper identity, temptation, effort, brand switching, and trust in deal-finding tools, plus an importance battery on discount size, ease of redemption, planned purchases, data sharing, and expiration pressure. Further items cover coupon frustrations, loyalty programs, a top-3 ranking of what would drive more use, motivation trends, satisfaction, recommendation likelihood, a 0-10 next-purchase intent scale, and open numeric and text fields on monthly coupons, savings, and habits. It ends with a demographic block including age, gender, race or ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, community type, and shopping style.
