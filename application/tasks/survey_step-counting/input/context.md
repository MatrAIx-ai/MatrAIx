# Step Counting

Consumer use of daily step counting, including devices, goals, motivation, and satisfaction.

## About this survey

This survey measures whether and how long people count their daily steps, what device they use (smartwatch, fitness band, phone sensors, clip-on pedometer) and which apps they view data in (Apple Health, Fitbit, Google Fit, Samsung Health, Garmin Connect). It covers wearing habits, step goals, typical step counts, how often people check, feature use, and participation in step challenges. Attitudes are captured through agreement and importance Likert batteries covering motivation, accomplishment, trust in accuracy, goal pressure, habit formation, and what matters in a device, plus a "top 3" reasons ranking, data-privacy concern, behavior changes, overall satisfaction, a 0–10 recommendation scale, and future intent. Numeric free-text items capture yesterday's steps, current step goal, days the goal was met, and household size, followed by a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, marital status, household size, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural, and main tracking device owned).
