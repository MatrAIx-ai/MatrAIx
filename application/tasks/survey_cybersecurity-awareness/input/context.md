# Personal Cybersecurity Awareness & Habits

Everyday cybersecurity awareness, protective habits, experiences, and attitudes among ordinary technology users.

## About this survey

This survey gauges the respondent's self-rated security knowledge and level of concern, then maps their password management (including password managers), password reuse, two-factor authentication and second-factor methods, update habits, security tools (antivirus, VPN, firewall, encryption), and backup practices. It measures public Wi-Fi behavior, phone lock security, past security incidents, account counts, and breach-checking habits, followed by Likert agreement statements and a concern matrix split per item across identity theft, fraud, data collection, tracking, and AI-enabled scams. It also covers privacy habits, settings review, information sources, and awareness training, then closes with a top-3 best-practices ranking, recovery confidence, intent to improve, biggest barrier, and an open item. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, a concern matrix split per item, a ranking, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured include age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, whether they work in IT/security, household size, relationship status, device count, U.S. region, and urban/suburban/rural.
