# Surprise & Out-of-Network Billing

Consumers' experiences with, awareness of, and attitudes toward surprise and out-of-network medical bills.

## About this survey

This survey opens by gauging familiarity with surprise billing and whether respondents have received an unexpectedly high out-of-network bill, in which setting, and for how much. It measures preventive behaviors such as checking network status, how they verify coverage, and awareness of the No Surprises Act, along with the actions taken and outcomes when a surprise bill occurred. Attitudinal batteries capture agreement on fairness, confusion, trust in insurers, and whether emergencies make checking realistic, plus worry level and an importance matrix on desired protections. Later questions cover perceived responsibility, willingness to dispute, care avoidance, a top-three ranking of wanted protections, and closing satisfaction and word-of-mouth intent. Question types include single-choice, multi- select, several Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking, and numeric free-text items. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, marital status, region, community type, and primary insurance source.
