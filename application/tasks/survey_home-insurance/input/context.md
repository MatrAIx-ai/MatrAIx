# Home and Renters Insurance

How homeowners and renters obtain, understand, and evaluate their property insurance, and what drives their coverage and provider decisions.

## About this survey

This survey screens for housing situation and insurance status, then captures the provider, premium, tenure, how the policy was obtained, coverage components, deductibles, and separate flood coverage. It measures replacement-cost confidence, decision drivers through an importance battery (price, coverage, reputation, claims, bundling), and worries about specific perils, along with risk-reduction upgrades and claims history. Attitude scales probe policy understanding, value, premium anxiety, and home inventories, while a top-3 ranking captures switching triggers. Later sections cover premium changes, recent shopping, bundling, non-renewals, satisfaction, future intent, and a likelihood-to-recommend scale, with numeric free-text for premium and household size. A comprehensive demographics block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, marital status, region, community type, home type, and mortgage status.
