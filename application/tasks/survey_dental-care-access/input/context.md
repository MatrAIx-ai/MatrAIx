# Dental Care Access

Consumer access to dental care, including finding a dentist, affording visits, and the barriers that lead people to delay or skip care.

## About this survey

This survey measures how recently and regularly respondents visit a dentist, whether they have a regular provider, how they found one, and what services they have had. It covers how people pay for care and out-of-pocket spending, barriers such as cost, lack of insurance, availability, distance, and dental anxiety, and Likert batteries on attitudes toward dental care plus a matrix rating what matters when choosing a dentist. It also captures delayed or skipped care, emergency dental visits, self-rated oral health, travel and wait times, confidence in affording bills, satisfaction and quality ratings, a likelihood-to-recommend scale, and a top-3 ranking of what would improve access. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, a matrix battery split per item, numeric free-text items, and a demographic block covering age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, area type, dental coverage, and self-rated health.
