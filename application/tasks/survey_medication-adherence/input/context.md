# Medication Routine & Adherence

How consumers organize their daily medication routines and stay on track with taking them as directed.

## About this survey

This survey screens for regular medication use, how many are taken daily, and how long they have been taken, then measures routine behaviors such as timing, reminder methods, pill organizers, apps and smart dispensers, and how consistently doses are taken. It explores reasons for missed doses, what to do after a missed dose, refill tracking, and running out, and includes agreement batteries on routine reliability, ease of remembering, complexity of multiple medications, understanding each medication, and confidence in self-management. An importance matrix rates what helps most in staying on track, followed by interest in support tools, a self-rated adherence score, a top-three ranking of helpful changes, and closing satisfaction. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking, and numeric free-text items. The content is framed around habits and tools rather than any specific medical condition. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, marital status, region, community type, and primary insurance source.
