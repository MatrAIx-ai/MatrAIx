# Phishing & Online Scam Awareness

How aware people are of phishing and online scams, how they respond to suspicious messages, and how protected they feel.

## About this survey

This survey measures the respondent's familiarity with phishing and confidence in spotting it, then maps how often and through which channels they receive suspicious messages, the scam types they have encountered, and the cues they check to judge legitimacy. It captures whether they have clicked a scam link or suffered loss, how they respond to suspected phishing, reporting habits, and estimated weekly volume, followed by Likert agreement statements and two matrices split per item: confidence identifying specific scam scenarios and worry about oneself and vulnerable relatives. It also covers protective measures, training, and information sources, then closes with a top-3 warning-signs ranking, perceived change in scam volume, likelihood of verifying urgent messages, overall sense of protection, and an open item. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, matrices split per item, a ranking, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured include age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, whether they work in IT/security, comfort with technology, household size, relationship status, U.S. region, and urban/suburban/rural.
