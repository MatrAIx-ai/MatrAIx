# Life Insurance Attitudes

How people think about, own, and make decisions about life insurance, including coverage adequacy, barriers, and trust in advice sources.

## About this survey

This survey screens for life insurance ownership and type (term, whole, group, final expense), then captures tenure, coverage amount, premium, provider, and how the policy was obtained. It records who depends on the respondent's income and uses a top-3 ranking for motivations, an importance battery for decision factors (cost, coverage, reputation, cash value, ease), and agreement scales measuring adequacy, cost perceptions, understanding, discomfort, and norms. Additional sections cover confidence in coverage needs, barriers to buying, review habits, triggering life events, a trust battery across advice sources, satisfaction, purchase intent, and a likelihood-to-recommend scale, with numeric free-text for coverage amount, premium, household size, and children. A comprehensive demographics block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, marital status, region, community type, homeownership, and primary-earner status.
