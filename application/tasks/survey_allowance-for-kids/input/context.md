# Allowance for Kids

How parents use allowances and everyday practices to teach their children about money.

## About this survey

This survey screens for parents of children aged 4 to 17 and asks whether and how they give an allowance, including the starting age, frequency, amount, and whether it is tied to chores, grades, or age. It records how the allowance is delivered (cash, kids' debit apps, accounts, or jars) and which money skills parents actively teach. Attitudes are captured through agreement batteries on responsibility, earning versus giving, spending freedom, and comfort discussing money, and an importance battery on saving, earning, giving, and independent decision-making. Further items cover saving requirements and matching, money-management tools, the biggest teaching challenge, a top-3 money-lessons ranking, confidence, satisfaction, allowance intent, and an open comment field. It ends with a demographic block including age, gender, race or ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, number of children, marital status, region, community type, and the respondent's role in household money decisions.
