# Health Screening Participation

Consumer participation in routine health screenings, including blood pressure, cholesterol, blood sugar, and age-appropriate screening tests, and the barriers that affect it.

## About this survey

This survey measures how up to date respondents are on screenings, which tests they have had recently, where they get screened, and how they learn a test is due. It includes Likert batteries on attitudes toward screening (early detection, comfort, understanding results, worry) and a matrix rating how much barriers such as cost, time, knowledge, fear, and transportation affect participation. It also covers follow-up on abnormal results, how results are received, at-home testing, importance and likelihood of future screening, satisfaction and experience ratings, a likelihood-to-recommend scale, and a top-3 ranking of influencing factors. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, a matrix battery split per item, numeric free-text items, and a demographic block covering age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, area type, insurance, and self-rated health.
