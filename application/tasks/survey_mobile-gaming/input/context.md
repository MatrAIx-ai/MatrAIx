# Mobile Gaming

How people play games on smartphones and tablets, including their genres, spending, monetization attitudes, and playing habits.

## About this survey

This survey covers mobile gaming behavior: how long and how much respondents play, their main device, number of installed games, and when they play. It measures preferred genres through a top-3 ranking, how games are discovered, payment behavior, yearly spending, and what people buy in-app, along with playing behaviors (online play, rewarded ads, daily streaks, uninstalling) through a frequency battery. Attitudes toward relaxation, ad frequency, spending pressure, overplaying, and mobile-versus-console are measured through Likert scales, and an importance battery covers what people value in a game, alongside subscription use, solo-versus-social play, reactions to aggressive monetization, satisfaction, and future intent. The question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert and importance batteries, a ranking question, a 0–10 recommendation scale, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, U.S. region, and urban/suburban/rural setting.
