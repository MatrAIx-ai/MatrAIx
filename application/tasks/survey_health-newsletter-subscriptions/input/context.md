# Health Newsletter Subscriptions

How consumers subscribe to, read, trust, and value email and digital health and wellness newsletters.

## About this survey

This survey measures whether and how often people subscribe to health and wellness newsletters, how many they receive, how long they have followed them, and how frequently they open and read them. It captures the topics and publishers they follow, how they discovered subscriptions, preferred formats, devices, and reading times, and whether subscriptions are free or paid. An agreement-scale attitude battery covers trustworthiness, clarity, relevance, frequency, actionability, influence on decisions, and advertising, alongside satisfaction, importance, action-taking and sharing behavior, trust relative to a doctor, fact-checking habits, unsubscribe triggers, a top-three topic ranking, personalization and privacy attitudes, a 0–10 recommendation scale, and future-intent and willingness-to-pay items, plus open comments and numeric free-text on counts, spend, and reading time. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, employment, income, marital status, household size, region, urban or rural setting, and overall health-information interest. Content is framed attitudinally and kept consumer-oriented.
