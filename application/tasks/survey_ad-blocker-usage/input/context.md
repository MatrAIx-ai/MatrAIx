# Ad Blocker Usage

How people use ad blockers and other tools to manage online advertising across browsers, apps, and devices.

## About this survey

This survey explores ad-blocking behavior: whether respondents block ads, what prompted them, and which tools they rely on (uBlock Origin, AdBlock, Adblock Plus, AdGuard, Ghostery, Brave, Pi-hole, and browser built-ins). It measures where and on which devices they block ads, paid ad-free subscriptions and spending, and whitelisting habits, then uses Likert batteries on annoyance, privacy, and creator support plus a matrix rating how much different ad types bother them. Additional sections cover anti-adblock walls, reasons for abandoning blockers, acceptance of non-intrusive ads, companion privacy tools, a top-3 reasons ranking, and outcome measures for satisfaction, likelihood to recommend, and future intent. The demographic block captures age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urbanicity, tech comfort, and relationship to tech work.
