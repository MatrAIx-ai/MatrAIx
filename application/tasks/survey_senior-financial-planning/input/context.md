# Senior Financial Planning

How older adults plan and manage their finances in and around retirement.

## About this survey

This survey identifies the respondent's retirement stage and planned retirement age, then measures financial preparedness, expected income sources (Social Security, pensions, 401(k)s, IRAs, savings, annuities, real estate), and whether they have a written plan or professional help. Likert batteries assess confidence in lifestyle, worry about outliving money, healthcare-cost concern, sense of control, and inheritance intent, and a concern matrix rates specific retirement risks including running out of savings, medical and long-term-care costs, inflation, being a burden, and scams. Further items cover estate and later-life preparations, drawdown strategy, investment allocation, Social Security claiming, housing plans, help with money tasks, and online-scam confidence. Outcome questions include a top-three priorities ranking, review frequency, debt, satisfaction, and likelihood of changing the plan, with free-text items for retirement age, total savings, and household size. A full demographic block records age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, household size, marital status, region, urbanicity, homeownership, and type of health coverage.
