# Financial Confidence

How confident and secure people feel about managing their money, handling setbacks, and reaching their financial goals.

## About this survey

This survey opens with overall ratings of financial confidence, current situation, and how confidence has changed over the past year, then identifies areas where respondents feel confident or anxious. A confidence battery rates specific domains such as handling an unexpected expense, saving for retirement, investing, understanding products, and staying on track for goals, while agreement and frequency Likert scales capture sense of control, money anxiety, comfort talking about money, and worry frequency. Behavioral items cover budgeting, emergency funds, goal-setting, and account-checking habits, plus free-text measures of months of expenses covered and a 1–10 stress rating. A matrix on drivers of confidence, a top-3 ranking of what would improve it, and questions on knowledge, advice sources, and professional help round out the attitudes. Outcome items measure optimism, likelihood to take action, satisfaction, and a 0–10 security scale, with an optional open comment. A comprehensive demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, education, employment, income, marital status, household size, dependents, region, and urbanicity.
