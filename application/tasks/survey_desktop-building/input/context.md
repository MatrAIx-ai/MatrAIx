# Desktop PC Building

Building, upgrading, and owning custom desktop PCs — experience level, component choices, budgets, build resources, and satisfaction among DIY and enthusiast builders.

## About this survey

This survey profiles people who build or upgrade their own desktop computers. It screens for build experience and recency, then captures primary uses (gaming, development, editing, rendering, ML, home server), reasons for building over buying, spend and budget, CPU and GPU brand preferences, where parts are bought, and the planning resources used (PCPartPicker, YouTube, Reddit, benchmarks). Importance batteries rate criteria such as price-to-performance, graphics, cooling, aesthetics, upgradability, and efficiency, plus a top-3 components-to-splurge-on ranking and detail on cooling, storage, and RAM. Agreement batteries probe cost savings, enjoyment of the process, troubleshooting confidence, staying current, and GPU-price barriers, followed by upgrade cadence, build problems encountered, overclocking, OS, satisfaction, future-build intent, and a 0–10 recommendation score. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, importance/agreement/satisfaction scales, and numeric and open free-text. Demographics include age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, region, urban/rural, and self-rated hardware-enthusiast level.
