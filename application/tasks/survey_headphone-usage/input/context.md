# Headphone Usage

Everyday use of headphones and earbuds — device types and brands, listening habits, features valued, and attitudes toward sound quality and hearing safety.

## About this survey

This survey covers how people use headphones and earbuds day to day. It captures usage frequency, types and brands owned, the type used most, number of pairs, primary uses (music, podcasts, calls, gaming, exercise, focus), daily wearing hours, where they are used, connection type, noise-cancellation ownership, and spend. Importance batteries rate sound quality, comfort, noise cancellation, battery life, price, brand, and call quality, followed by a top-3 features ranking. Agreement batteries probe paying for sound quality, dependence, hearing-damage concern, noise-cancellation benefit, and true-wireless preference, while further questions cover high-volume listening, hearing-safety features, replacement cadence and triggers, frustrations, satisfaction, purchase intent, and a 0–10 recommendation score. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, importance/agreement/frequency/satisfaction scales, and numeric and open free-text. Demographics include age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, region, urban/rural, smartphone platform, and self-described audio-enthusiast level.
