# Mail-Order Pharmacy Usage

How consumers use mail-order and home-delivery pharmacies and how they weigh them against local pharmacies.

## About this survey

This survey screens for whether respondents receive prescriptions by mail, which service they use, how they started, and which medications they receive that way. It measures behaviors such as delivery frequency, supply length, refill management, and problems experienced (late or lost packages, temperature concerns, running out), plus estimated monthly savings. Agreement batteries cover convenience, savings, trust in safe delivery, missing in-person pharmacists, and packaging worries, and an importance matrix rates what matters most in a mail-order service. Later questions compare mail order with local pharmacies, identify situations where a local pharmacy is still preferred, and include a top-three ranking of priorities before closing satisfaction, likelihood to recommend, and intent to continue. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking, and numeric free-text items. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, marital status, region, community type, and primary insurance source.
