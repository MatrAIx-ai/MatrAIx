# Data Backup Habits

How people back up, protect, and recover their personal and work data.

## About this survey

This survey opens with a screener on whether and how often the respondent backs up, past data-loss experiences, and whether they have ever verified a restore, then details the methods, cloud services, and local tools they use, the data types and devices they cover, and whether they follow the 3-2-1 rule with off-site or offline copies. Attitudes are measured with an agreement battery (recovery confidence, hassle, ransomware worry, automation, cloud privacy, and trust), an importance matrix for what backups protect against, and a top-three ranking of reasons to back up, plus barriers to backing up better and encryption practices. Outcome items cover satisfaction, disaster-recovery confidence, intent to improve, and likelihood to recommend, using single-choice, multi-select, Likert, matrix, ranking, and numeric free-text formats. The demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, relationship status, region, urban/suburban/rural setting, technical role, and overall tech comfort.
