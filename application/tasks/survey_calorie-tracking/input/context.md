# Calorie Tracking

Consumer habits, tools, motivations, and satisfaction around tracking calories and food intake.

## About this survey

This survey measures whether and how long people track their calories, the method they use (app, journal, spreadsheet, mental estimate) and which apps (MyFitnessPal, Lose It!, Cronometer, Noom, Apple Health), how they log foods, how much of their day they log, and their goals and targets. It covers additional nutrients tracked, exercise-calorie logging, useful app features, and the main challenges of tracking. Attitudes are captured through agreement and importance Likert batteries covering better choices, effort, trust, stress, goal progress, portion awareness, and what matters in a tool, plus a "top 3" reasons ranking, premium-subscription and data-privacy questions, overall satisfaction, a 0–10 recommendation scale, and future intent. Numeric free-text items capture daily calorie target, time spent logging, days logged, and household size, followed by a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, marital status, household size, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural, and home meal preparation).
