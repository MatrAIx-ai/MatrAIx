# Charitable Giving

How people donate money and time to charitable causes and choose organizations.

## About this survey

This survey covers whether and how much people give, giving frequency, share of income donated, and the causes they support, from religious organizations and food banks to disaster relief, health research, and animal welfare. It measures donation channels (online, crowdfunding, recurring gifts, payroll giving, checkout round-ups, donor-advised funds), volunteering, in-kind support, and how donors research charities. Likert and importance batteries capture giving identity, trust, tax motivations, and the factors that drive charity choice such as overhead, impact, transparency, and locality, plus a ranked set of giving motivations. Closing items probe itemizing, expected changes in giving, economic effects, satisfaction, and intent. A full demographic block records age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, religiosity, marital status, household size, region, and urbanicity.
