# Patient Portal Usage

Consumer use of, experience with, and satisfaction with online patient portals for managing care.

## About this survey

This survey screens for portal access and account status, identifies which portal systems people use (MyChart, FollowMyHealth, insurer and pharmacy portals, and others), and measures how many portals they hold, how often and how they log in, and login counts over the past year. It inventories the tasks people use portals for, their most frequent task, ease of use, and satisfaction with message responses. Attitudinal Likert batteries cover manageability, findability, understandability, data security, and multi-portal frustration, and a rated matrix battery scores satisfaction with results, messaging, scheduling, refills, and billing. Later sections examine viewing results before a provider discussion, managing portals for others, barriers to use, record-aggregation apps, a top-3 ranking of desired improvements, overall satisfaction, future intent, and a 0–10 recommendation score. It uses single-choice, multi-select, Likert and satisfaction scales, a matrix battery, and numeric free-text, and closes with demographics covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, technology comfort, insurance status, household size, region, urbanicity, and income.
