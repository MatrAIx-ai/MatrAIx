# VR and AR Adoption

How people adopt and use virtual reality (VR) and augmented reality (AR) technology, including headset ownership, use cases, comfort, and attitudes toward the technology.

## About this survey

This survey covers VR and AR adoption: respondents' experience and ownership, which headsets they have used, use cases, weekly hours, spending, and where they use the technology. It measures common problems (motion sickness, comfort, eye strain, battery, content gaps) through a frequency battery, and attitudes toward potential, cost, comfort, content, mainstream adoption, and privacy through Likert scales. A top-3 ranking captures barriers to greater use, and an importance battery covers what people value in a headset, alongside setup type, phone-based AR use, purchase interest and willingness to pay, satisfaction, and future intent. The question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert and importance batteries, matrix-style frequency items, a ranking question, a 0–10 recommendation scale, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, presence of children, U.S. region, and urban/suburban/rural setting.
