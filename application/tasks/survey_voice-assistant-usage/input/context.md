# Voice Assistant Usage

How people use voice assistants such as Alexa, Google Assistant, and Siri — which assistants and devices they use, what tasks they perform, and their attitudes toward accuracy, privacy, and trust.

## About this survey

This survey examines everyday voice-assistant behavior and sentiment. After a usage screener and tenure question, it captures which assistants people use (Alexa, Google Assistant, Siri, Bixby, AI chatbot voice modes, in-car assistants), the devices they use them on, frequency, primary tasks, and a top-3 ranking of most valued uses. Likert batteries measure agreement on time savings, recognition accuracy, answer quality, always-listening concern, and data trust; a performance battery rates how well the assistant handles simple, complex, accented, and follow-up requests. It also probes privacy-setting behavior, frustrations, interest in newer AI-powered assistants, satisfaction, retention likelihood, and a 0–10 recommendation score. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, agreement/importance/frequency/quality scales, and numeric and open free-text. Demographics include age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, children in home, region, urban/rural, smartphone platform, and tech comfort.
