# Hearing Health

Consumer hearing health habits and attitudes, including hearing checks, noise exposure, and views on hearing care and devices.

## About this survey

This survey measures respondents' self-rated hearing, whether and where they have had a hearing test, and how often they have trouble hearing across everyday situations (captured as a matrix battery). It covers use of hearing devices, exposure to loud environments, use of hearing protection, and headphone habits. Likert batteries measure attitudes toward protecting hearing, stigma, cost, and over-the-counter options, and a second matrix rates what matters when choosing a device. It also covers noticed hearing difficulty, ringing in the ears, barriers to seeking care, confidence in knowing where to get help, likelihood of a future test, overall satisfaction, a likelihood-to-encourage-others scale, and a top-3 ranking of what would encourage hearing care. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, two matrix batteries split per item, a numeric free-text item, and a demographic block covering age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, area type, insurance, and self-rated health.
