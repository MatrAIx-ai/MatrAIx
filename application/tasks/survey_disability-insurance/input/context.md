# Disability Insurance

Ownership of, attitudes toward, and confidence in disability insurance as a way to protect household income against illness or injury.

## About this survey

This survey screens whether respondents hold short-term, long-term, employer-group, or individual disability coverage, how they obtained it, the income-replacement percentage, and what they pay in premiums. It measures perceived risk of a lasting disability, how long a household could last without the respondent's income, and understanding of how coverage works. Attitudes are captured through agreement batteries on income dependence, cost, and reliance on employer or Social Security coverage, and a concern battery covering injury, serious illness, mental health, maternity gaps, and job-change coverage loss. Further items cover a top-3 purchase-driver ranking, reasons for not buying more coverage, claim experience, information sources, satisfaction, purchase intent, and a 0-10 income-shock confidence scale, along with occupation risk, breadwinner share, and an open comment field. It ends with a full demographic block including age, gender, race or ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, number of dependents, marital status, region, community type, and homeownership.
