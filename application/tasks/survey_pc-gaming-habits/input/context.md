# PC Gaming Habits

How people play video games on a personal computer, including their hardware, storefronts, spending, and gaming behaviors.

## About this survey

This survey covers PC gaming behavior: how long and how much respondents play, the kind of PC and storefronts they use, library size, input devices, and setup components. It measures preferred genres through a top-3 ranking, play style, spending and acquisition methods, subscription use, and social behaviors (playing with friends, voice chat, watching streams, mods, ranked play) through a frequency battery. Attitudes toward gaming as a hobby, PC-versus-console, upgrade costs, and microtransactions are measured through Likert scales, and an importance battery covers game-purchase drivers, alongside hardware-upgrade habits, content creation, satisfaction, and future intent. The question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert and importance batteries, a ranking question, a 0–10 recommendation scale, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, U.S. region, and urban/suburban/rural setting.
