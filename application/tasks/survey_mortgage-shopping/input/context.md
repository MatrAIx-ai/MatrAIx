# Mortgage Shopping

How people compare lenders and shop for a mortgage.

## About this survey

This survey explores how respondents shop for a mortgage — loan purpose, loan type (conventional, FHA, VA, USDA, jumbo), and term — and how many and which kinds of lenders they compared, from credit unions and national banks to online lenders like Rocket Mortgage and Better.com and mortgage brokers. It measures resources and tools used, pre-approval, rate locks, discount points, and the fees compared. Likert batteries capture attitudes toward comparison shopping, confusion, confidence, trust, and affordability worry, plus the importance of rate, closing costs, speed, service, and reputation, while multi-select and top-3 items cover lender types, tools, fees compared, and advice for first-time shoppers. Numeric free-text records interest rate, loan amount, and closing costs, and a full demographic block captures age, gender, race or ethnicity, income, education, employment, self-reported credit score range, household size, marital status, region, community type, and homeownership.
