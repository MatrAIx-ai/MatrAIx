# Clinic Wait Times

Consumer experience with, and attitudes toward, waiting for care at clinics and doctors' offices.

## About this survey

This survey measures how often people visit clinics, the type of clinic they use most, and how long they wait at each stage (waiting room, exam room, and past their appointment time), including their longest wait and how waits compare with expectations. It captures how wait time affects satisfaction, what people do while waiting, and whether they have left without being seen or switched clinics over waits. Attitudes are captured through agreement, frequency, and importance Likert batteries covering return likelihood, communication about delays, willingness to trade convenience for shorter waits, waiting-area comfort, expectations, and how often delays and updates occur, plus check-in methods, a "top 3" improvement ranking, overall satisfaction, a 0–10 recommendation scale, and retention intent. Numeric free-text items capture minutes waited, an acceptable-wait threshold, total visit duration, and household size, followed by a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, marital status, household size, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural, insurance type, and overall health).
