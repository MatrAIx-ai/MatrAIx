# Photo Management

How people store, organize, edit, back up, and share their personal photo collections.

## About this survey

This survey starts with a screener on collection size, capture devices, shooting frequency, and how actively the respondent organizes photos, then details where they store images, which apps and services they use (Apple Photos, Google Photos, Amazon Photos, Lightroom, NAS libraries, and others), their primary library, and their organizing, editing, sharing, and backup habits. Attitudes are measured with an agreement battery (organization, loss worry, duplicates, findability, overwhelm, AI usefulness, and printing), an importance matrix for photo-service selection factors, and a top-three ranking of valued features, plus questions on culling, AI search, concerns, and portability. Outcome items cover satisfaction, findability confidence, switching intent, and likelihood to recommend, using single-choice, multi-select, Likert, matrix, ranking, and numeric free-text formats. The demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, relationship status, region, urban/suburban/rural setting, photographer type, and overall tech comfort.
