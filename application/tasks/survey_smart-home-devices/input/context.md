# Smart Home Devices

Adoption and everyday use of smart home devices — which connected devices people own, the ecosystems and brands they choose, and their attitudes toward convenience, security, privacy, and cost.

## About this survey

This survey measures how consumers adopt and live with connected-home technology. It opens with a screener on ownership and tenure, then covers device types owned (speakers, lighting, cameras, locks, thermostats, robot vacuums, sensors, appliances), the primary platform (Alexa, Google Home, Apple HomeKit, SmartThings, Home Assistant), specific brands, control methods, and use of automations. Likert batteries capture agreement on convenience, reliability, ease of setup, privacy concern, energy savings, and hacking worries, plus importance ratings for purchase criteria, a top-3 benefits ranking, satisfaction, and likelihood to add devices or recommend. It uses single-choice, multi-select, agreement/importance/frequency/satisfaction scales, a 0–10 recommendation item, and numeric and open free-text. Demographics include age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, region, urban/rural, homeownership, home type, and tech comfort.
