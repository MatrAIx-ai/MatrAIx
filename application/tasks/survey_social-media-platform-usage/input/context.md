# Social Media Platform Usage

How people use social media platforms, including which platforms they favor, how they engage, and their attitudes toward time, privacy, and wellbeing.

## About this survey

This survey covers social media usage: which platforms respondents have accounts on, which they use daily, their most-used platform, time spent, and primary purposes. It measures engagement behaviors (posting, liking, commenting, sharing, messaging, short-form video) through a frequency battery, and attitudes toward connection, overuse, mood, data privacy, social comparison, and trust through Likert scales. It also covers privacy actions, digital-wellbeing habits, news consumption, social commerce, influencer following, a top-3 content-type ranking, professional use, satisfaction, and future intent. The question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert and frequency batteries, a ranking question, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, relationship status, U.S. region, and urban/suburban/rural setting.
