# Options Trading

How individuals engage with stock and ETF options trading — their strategies, platforms, risk discipline, and outcomes.

## About this survey

This survey opens with a screener on whether, how long, and how often the respondent trades options, along with their account approval level and portfolio allocation. It then captures detailed behaviors: the brokerages they use (Robinhood, Fidelity, thinkorswim, Tastytrade, Interactive Brokers, and others), the strategies they employ (buying calls/puts, covered calls, spreads, iron condors, naked options), the underlyings they trade, typical position size and monthly trade count, use of 0DTE contracts and margin, learning sources, and analysis tools. Likert batteries measure trading discipline and self-perceived skill, an importance battery rates platform-selection criteria, and a "top 3" ranking identifies what drives trade entry. Sub-topic questions cover assignment and total-loss experiences, monitoring habits, emotional decision-making, and worst-case losses, followed by profitability, platform satisfaction, and future intent including a 0–10 recommendation scale. It closes with a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urban/rural, and investable assets).
