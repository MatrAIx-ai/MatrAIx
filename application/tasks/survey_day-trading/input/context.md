# Day Trading

How people engage in active and day trading of stocks and other assets, and how it affects them.

## About this survey

This survey screens for active and day-trading activity and captures tenure, trading days per week, number of trades, hours spent, assets traded (stocks, options, ETFs, crypto, futures, forex), and platforms used (Robinhood, Webull, Fidelity, thinkorswim, Interactive Brokers, crypto exchanges). It records capital deployed, goals, strategies, margin use, results, and account return. Behavioral and risk items cover trading plans, stop-losses, and loss-chasing. Agreement batteries measure discipline, emotional trading, self-assessed skill, time demands, and stress, and a frequency matrix tracks emotions felt while trading (excitement, stress, regret, confidence). Additional items cover information sources, pattern-day-trader-rule awareness, risk tolerance, and confidence, plus a top-3 ranking of success factors. It closes with changing activity, satisfaction, forward plans, a likelihood-to-recommend score, and an open comment. A comprehensive demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, and area type so personas can be matched.
