# First-Aid Preparedness

How prepared households are for everyday first-aid situations and minor emergencies.

## About this survey

This survey measures household preparedness for everyday first aid and minor emergencies. It opens with screener questions on whether people keep a first-aid kit, where supplies are stored, and how recently they were restocked, then inventories specific first-aid and emergency items on hand and training history. Confidence items rate readiness to handle cuts, burns, sprains, CPR, and recognizing when professional care is needed, while Likert batteries capture perceived preparedness and organization, and an importance battery rates preparedness priorities. A top-3 ranking identifies the most essential kit items, and further questions cover usage, guidance sources, first-aid apps, spending, overall preparedness, and interest in training. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, presence of children, marital status, U.S. region, community type, and home type. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several rating scales, a matrix battery, a ranking item, and numeric free-text.
