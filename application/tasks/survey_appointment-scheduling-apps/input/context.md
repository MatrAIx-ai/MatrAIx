# Appointment Scheduling Apps

Consumer use of, experience with, and attitudes toward booking healthcare appointments online through apps and websites.

## About this survey

This survey measures how people book healthcare appointments, whether and how long they have booked online, and which tools they use (patient portals like MyChart, hospital apps, Zocdoc, pharmacy and clinic apps). It covers the types of appointments booked online, devices used, features used (booking, rescheduling, reminders, calendar sync, pre-visit forms, waitlists, family booking), and reminder preferences. Attitudes are captured through agreement, satisfaction, and importance Likert batteries covering time savings, ease, trust, privacy, missed-appointment reduction, and specific app aspects, plus problems experienced, fallback behavior, a "top 3" feature ranking, missed-appointment history, overall satisfaction, a 0–10 recommendation scale, and future intent. Numeric free-text items capture appointments booked, booking time, days booked in advance, and household size, followed by a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, marital status, household size, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural, and general comfort with apps).
