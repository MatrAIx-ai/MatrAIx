# Financial Fraud Experience

People's real-world experiences with financial scams and fraud, the losses and responses involved, and how they protect themselves.

## About this survey

This survey begins by screening whether the respondent has been targeted by or lost money to a scam in the past three years, then details the types of fraud encountered (phishing, imposter, romance, investment, tech-support, marketplace, and account fraud), the contact channels used, and the specifics of their most significant incident including amount lost, payment method, and resolution. It records the protective actions taken afterward and the security measures currently in place, along with how often statements are reviewed. Likert batteries measure fraud-related worry, trust in banks, and reactions to emerging AI-enabled scams; a concern battery rates specific scam categories; and a "top 3" ranking identifies the warning signs respondents rely on. Further questions cover scam-avoidance education, views on who bears responsibility, trends in scam volume, emotional impact, satisfaction with how institutions handled the case, and future protective intent. It closes with a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urban/rural, and technology comfort).
