# IRA Contributions

How individuals open, fund, and invest through individual retirement accounts (IRAs).

## About this survey

This survey examines IRA ownership and contribution behavior from account types and providers through how and how much people contribute — annual amounts, whether they hit the limit, contribution cadence, total balances, and how the money is invested. It measures behaviors such as Roth conversions, reviewing investments, and reasons for using an IRA alongside a workplace plan, then uses agreement and importance Likert batteries to capture understanding, consistency, confidence, and the role of tax benefits. Sub-topics include barriers to contributing more, retirement confidence, and likelihood of contributing again, followed by provider satisfaction, a 0-to-10 recommendation score, a top-3 reasons ranking, and an optional comment. A full demographic block records age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural setting, and workplace-plan access.
