# Clipboard Manager Usage

How people use clipboard managers — tools that keep a searchable history of copied items.

## About this survey

This survey begins with a screener on whether and how often the respondent uses a clipboard manager, how long they have used one, and their operating system, then details which tools they have used (Windows Clipboard History, macOS built-in, Paste, Maccy, Alfred, Raycast, Ditto, CopyQ, and others), their primary tool, what they paste from history, the features and devices they use, and how they discovered it. Attitudes are measured with an agreement battery (time savings, dependence, security worry, ease of use, OS built-in sufficiency, and cross-device sync), a security-concern item, an importance matrix for clipboard features, and a top-three ranking of reasons to use one, plus frustrations, text-expansion use, and payment. Outcome items cover satisfaction, productivity impact, retention intent, and likelihood to recommend, using single-choice, multi-select, Likert, matrix, ranking, and numeric free-text formats. The demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, relationship status, region, urban/suburban/rural setting, technical role, and overall tech comfort.
