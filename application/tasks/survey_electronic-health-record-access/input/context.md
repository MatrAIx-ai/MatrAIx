# Electronic Health Record Access

Consumer experience accessing, downloading, sharing, and controlling their own electronic health records.

## About this survey

This survey measures whether respondents know they can access their electronic health records, whether they have viewed or downloaded them, and how often, along with the channels used and the parts of the record examined. It captures downloading and sharing behavior, the purposes people put records to, experiences finding and correcting errors, ease of access, and the problems encountered. Attitudinal Likert batteries cover sense of control, understandability, single-point-of-access importance, privacy worry, and care improvement, and a rated matrix battery scores comfort with provider-to-provider sharing, electronic storage, third-party app sharing, and de-identified research use. Later sections cover importance and confidence, interest in record-aggregation apps, a top-3 ranking of desired improvements, overall satisfaction, and an optional open comment. It uses single-choice, multi-select, Likert and comfort scales, a matrix battery, and numeric free-text, and closes with demographics covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, technology comfort, employment, insurance status, household size, region, urbanicity, and income.
