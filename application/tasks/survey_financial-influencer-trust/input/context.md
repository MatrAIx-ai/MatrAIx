# Financial Influencer Trust

How people follow and trust financial influencers ("finfluencers") on social media and how their content affects money decisions.

## About this survey

This survey screens for whether people follow financial influencers and how often they encounter such content, then captures the platforms (YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, X, Reddit), content types, and how long they have followed creators. Behavioral items cover actions taken on influencer advice — from budgeting and opening accounts to buying specific stocks or crypto and purchasing courses. Likert batteries assess attitudes about clarity, sales motives, learning, money anxiety, and the ability to spot bad advice, and an importance matrix rates trust factors such as credentials, sponsorship disclosure, track record, authenticity, follower count, and corroboration with other sources. Further questions address verification habits, scam concern, past losses, comparison with traditional sources, most-trusted guidance, a top-three ranking of trust-building factors, and overall helpfulness and satisfaction, with free-text items for number of influencers followed and household size. A full demographic block records age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, marital status, region, urbanicity, social-media use, and self-rated financial knowledge.
