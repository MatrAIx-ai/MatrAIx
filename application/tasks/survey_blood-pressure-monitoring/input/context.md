# Home Blood Pressure Monitoring

Consumer use of home blood pressure monitoring devices and how people track and act on their readings.

## About this survey

This survey screens for whether respondents check their blood pressure outside a doctor's office, whether they own a home monitor, and the device type and brand (Omron, Withings, iHealth, and others). It measures how long and how often they check, weekly reading counts, reasons for monitoring, app connectivity, and how readings are tracked and shared with a doctor. Further questions cover confidence taking readings, trust in device accuracy, and device cost. Attitudinal Likert batteries capture health awareness, between-visit value, monitoring anxiety, ease of use, and app helpfulness, and a rated usefulness matrix scores automatic saving, trend charts, doctor sharing, reminders, and display clarity. Later sections examine barriers, responses to unexpected readings, perceived benefit, a top-3 ranking of desired improvements, satisfaction, future intent, and a 0–10 recommendation score. It uses single-choice, multi-select, Likert and usefulness scales, a matrix battery, and numeric free-text, and closes with demographics covering age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, technology comfort, insurance status, household size, region, urbanicity, and income.
