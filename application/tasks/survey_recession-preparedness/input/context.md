# Recession Preparedness

How prepared households feel for an economic downturn and the steps they take to protect their finances.

## About this survey

This survey opens by measuring perceived recession likelihood, overall preparedness, prior recession experience, and how long a household could cover expenses if income stopped. It then captures concrete behaviors — preparedness steps taken, expenses people would cut first, and emergency cash sources — along with recent changes in spending and saving and the amount of high-interest debt carried. Several Likert batteries gauge attitudes about planning, anxiety, job confidence, and living paycheck to paycheck, and a matrix battery rates worry across specific downturn risks (job loss, housing, investments, prices, debt, family support). Additional items cover future plans, a top-three priorities ranking, investing behavior, re-employment confidence, safety nets, trust in government support, and overall satisfaction, with free-text items for months of savings, cash on hand, and a preparedness regret. A comprehensive demographic block records age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urbanicity, homeownership, and dependents.
