# Gadget Upgrade Cycle

How often people replace their personal tech devices — phones, laptops, tablets, wearables, and audio gear — and what drives those upgrade decisions.

## About this survey

This survey measures upgrade cadence across device types, the age of respondents' current phones, and the triggers that prompt a replacement, from breakage and slowdowns to new features and trade-in deals. It covers how people research and where they buy, how they pay, what they do with old devices, and whether they repair, insure, or pre-order. A per-device matrix captures replacement frequency for smartphones, laptops, tablets, headphones, and wearables, while Likert agreement and purchase-factor importance batteries gauge attitudes toward novelty, necessity, e-waste, value, and social pressure. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, split matrix batteries, a top-3 factor ranking, numeric and open free-text, plus satisfaction, upgrade-likelihood, and future-frequency items, followed by a full demographic block covering age, gender, race/ethnicity, education, employment, tech-consumer type, income, household, relationship status, region, and area type.
