# Health Literacy

How confident and comfortable people are finding, understanding, and using health information.

## About this survey

This survey assesses consumers' self-reported health literacy: their confidence and comfort in finding, understanding, and acting on health information. It opens with questions on where people look for health information and how often, then measures confidence across tasks such as filling out forms, reading prescription labels, understanding test results, and navigating insurance. A matrix battery rates how often various materials are hard to understand, and Likert items capture skills, source evaluation, decision confidence, and comfort asking questions. A trust battery compares sources, a top-3 ranking identifies areas people most want to improve, and further questions cover fact-checking habits, portal comfort, use of health apps and AI assistants, satisfaction, and interest in learning resources. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, community type, and primary language. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several rating scales, a matrix battery, a ranking item, and numeric free-text.
