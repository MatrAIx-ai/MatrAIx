# Certificate of Deposit

How people understand and use certificates of deposit (CDs) as a safe, fixed-term savings vehicle.

## About this survey

This survey screens for familiarity with CDs, current ownership, and recent CD openings, then explores where respondents open them (traditional banks, online banks, credit unions, brokerages), preferred term lengths, and reasons for use. It measures knowledge of CD concepts (early withdrawal penalties, laddering, no-penalty and bump-up CDs, brokered CDs, FDIC/NCUA insurance) and captures free-text amounts for total CD holdings, best rate seen, and comfortable deposit size. Likert batteries gauge attitudes about safety, penalties, opportunity cost versus stocks and bonds, and rate attractiveness, alongside an importance matrix on choosing a CD (APY, term, penalty, minimum deposit, insurance) and a top-3 ranking of reasons to prefer CDs. Additional items cover laddering, maturity decisions, comparisons with high-yield savings, information sources, likelihood to open a CD, satisfaction, a 0–10 recommendation score, and an optional comment. A comprehensive demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, education, employment, income, marital status, household size, life stage, region, and urbanicity.
