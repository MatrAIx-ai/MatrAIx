# Health Insurance Claim Experience

Consumer experience filing, tracking, and resolving health insurance claims.

## About this survey

This survey measures respondents' coverage and plan type, claim volume, who files their claims, how they submit or track them (insurer website, app, phone, mail, provider), and the kinds of care behind recent claims. It captures how well they understand coverage, the ease and speed of recent claims, denial and appeal experiences and responses, clarity of explanation-of-benefits statements, customer-service quality, and contact frequency. An agreement-scale attitude battery covers stress, clarity, speed, reachability, and fairness, alongside confidence in correct processing and understanding out-of-pocket costs, a top-three improvement ranking, the importance of the claims experience, overall satisfaction, a 0–10 recommendation scale, switching intent, and open comments. Numeric free-text captures claim count, contact count, and number of people covered. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, employment, marital status, income, region, and urban or rural setting. Content is framed attitudinally and kept consumer-oriented.
