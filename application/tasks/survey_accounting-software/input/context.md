# Accounting & Bookkeeping Software

How individuals, freelancers, and small businesses choose, use, and evaluate accounting and bookkeeping software.

## About this survey

This survey opens with a screener on the respondent's bookkeeping capacity, tenure, and usage frequency, then maps which tools they use (QuickBooks, Xero, FreshBooks, Wave, Sage, Zoho Books, NetSuite, spreadsheets, and others), their primary choice, and how they selected it. It measures accounting tasks performed, how transactions enter the system, devices and mobile use, integrations, weekly time spent, pricing model, and monthly spend, followed by Likert agreement and satisfaction batteries, an importance battery on selection criteria, confidence in accuracy, tax handling, and use of AI/automation features. It closes with a top-3 feature ranking, frustrations, switching behavior, a likelihood-to-recommend score, overall satisfaction, switch intent, and an open improvement item. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, a satisfaction matrix split per item, a ranking, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured include age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, business role, organization size, household size, U.S. region, and urban/suburban/rural.
