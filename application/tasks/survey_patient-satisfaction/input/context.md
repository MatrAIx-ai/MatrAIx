# Patient Satisfaction

Consumer satisfaction with the healthcare they receive, including provider, staff, access, and overall experience.

## About this survey

This survey measures how often people see a provider, the kind of provider they see, and how long they have seen them, along with an overall quality rating and satisfaction with their most recent visit. It uses frequency Likert batteries on provider communication behaviors (explaining, listening, respect, time, shared decisions, follow-up) and satisfaction batteries on staff courtesy, scheduling, wait time, cleanliness, and billing, plus agreement Likert items on trust, history, comfort, coordination, and dignity. It captures wait and access experience, between-visit communication channels, valued aspects of care, an importance battery, switching behavior, how people respond to good or bad experiences, a "top 3" priorities ranking, overall satisfaction, a 0–10 recommendation scale, and retention intent. Numeric free-text items capture minutes waited, visit length, days to get an appointment, and household size, followed by a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, marital status, household size, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural, insurance type, and overall health).
