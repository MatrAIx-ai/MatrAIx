# Vaccination Record Apps

How consumers use apps and digital tools to store, access, and share their vaccination and immunization records.

## About this survey

This survey measures how respondents keep track of immunization records, whether they use a dedicated vaccination record app or wallet, how long they have used one, which platform (patient portal, phone health wallet, public-health app, pharmacy app, standalone), what record types they store, and where the data comes from. It captures how they started, how often they check the app, situations that required showing a record, sharing methods, ease of use, and confidence in accuracy, together with an agreement-scale attitude battery on convenience, time savings, accuracy trust, privacy worry, and acceptance, plus a top-three feature ranking, family-record importance, privacy concern and security trust, difficulties, paper backups, reminder helpfulness, satisfaction, a 0–10 recommendation scale, future-use intent, and open comments. Numeric free-text captures how often records were shown, how many digital records are kept, and household size. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, employment, income, household size, presence of children, region, urban or rural setting, and overall comfort with health apps. Content is framed attitudinally and kept consumer-oriented.
