# Video Call Habits

How people use video calling and video conferencing across work and personal life, including the apps, devices, and features they rely on.

## About this survey

This survey covers video call behavior: how often respondents make calls, what they use them for, which apps and devices they favor, and the hardware and in-call features they use. It measures reliability and quality issues through a frequency battery, attitudes toward video calls (connection, fatigue, self-consciousness, effectiveness) via Likert scales, privacy practices, spending, and the factors that drive app choice through an importance battery. It also captures a top-3 ranking of frustrations, telehealth use, hosting behavior, satisfaction, likelihood to recommend, and expected future usage. The question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert and importance batteries, matrix-style frequency items, a ranking question, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, work arrangement, household size, U.S. region, and urban/suburban/rural setting.
