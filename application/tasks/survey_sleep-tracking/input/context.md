# Sleep Tracking

Consumer use of devices and apps to track and measure sleep.

## About this survey

This survey measures whether respondents track their sleep, for how long, and with what (Apple Watch, Fitbit, Oura, Whoop, phone apps such as Sleep Cycle, or bedside sensors), including which device they rely on most and what led them to start. It captures how often people check their data, which metrics and scores they follow, subscription use, and the habit changes they have made. Attitudes are measured through agreement, satisfaction, and importance batteries covering perceived benefit, data trust, behavior change, score anxiety, privacy, accuracy, clarity, insight usefulness, comfort, value, and desired features, plus frustrations, data-sharing with providers, churn, retention intent, a likelihood-to-recommend score, and a top-three improvements ranking. A comprehensive demographic block captures age band, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural setting, technology comfort, and self-rated health.
