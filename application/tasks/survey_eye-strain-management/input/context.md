# Eye Strain Management

Consumer experience of digital eye strain and the habits and products used to manage it.

## About this survey

This survey measures respondents' daily and work screen time, how often they experience eye strain, which symptoms they notice, which devices contribute most, and when strain tends to appear. It captures strain-reduction habits (breaks, the 20-20-20 rule, brightness and text adjustments, screen positioning), blue-light filters and glasses, comfort products, break-reminder apps, and the impact on comfort and productivity. An agreement-scale attitude battery covers active management, work constraints, perceived effect of blue-light products, break discipline, and sleep or evening comfort, alongside perceived effectiveness of breaks, eye-exam recency, corrective-lens use, professional consultation, barriers, a top-three approach ranking, importance, satisfaction, adoption intent, and open comments. Numeric free-text captures work screen hours and time-to-strain. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, employment, primary screen-use type, income, region, and urban or rural setting. Content is framed attitudinally and kept consumer-oriented.
