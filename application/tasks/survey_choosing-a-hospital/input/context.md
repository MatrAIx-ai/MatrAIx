# Choosing a Hospital

How consumers research, weigh factors, and decide when choosing a hospital for planned care.

## About this survey

This survey measures whether people have recently chosen a hospital for planned care, how the choice was made, the type of care involved, and how far they would travel for quality. It captures the sources they used to research hospitals (doctor recommendation, reviews, rating sites like Medicare Care Compare and Leapfrog, insurer directories, rankings), how much research they did, whether they checked network status, and how many hospitals they compared. Attitudes are captured through an importance battery of decision factors (quality, distance, network, doctor's advice, reputation, cost, procedure experience) and agreement Likert items on ratings, travel, comparability, cost, and reliance on doctors, plus feature preferences, hospital-type preference, barriers, a "top 3" priorities ranking, overall satisfaction, and a 0–10 recommendation scale. Numeric free-text items capture hospitals compared, distance to nearest hospital, travel willingness, and household size, followed by a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, marital status, household size, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural, insurance type, and overall health).
