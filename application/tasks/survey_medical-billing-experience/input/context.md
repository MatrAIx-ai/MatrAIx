# Medical Billing Experience

How consumers receive, understand, pay, and dispute their medical and dental bills.

## About this survey

This survey screens for recent medical bills and the types of care they came from, then measures billing behaviors such as how bills are received, how much is owed, payment methods, payment plans, reviewing itemized bills, and reading Explanation of Benefits statements. It probes experiences with billing errors, disputes, negotiating charges, and asking for cost estimates, and includes an agreement battery on bill clarity, predictability, trust, and whether billing worries affect care-seeking, plus an importance matrix on what makes a charge understandable. Later questions cover collections, payment preferences, help- seeking, and a top-three ranking of desired improvements, closing with satisfaction, likelihood to recommend, and confidence in catching errors. Question types include single- choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking, and numeric free-text items. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, marital status, region, community type, and primary insurance source.
