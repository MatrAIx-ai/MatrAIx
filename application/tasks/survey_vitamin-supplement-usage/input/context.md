# Vitamin & Supplement Usage

How consumers choose, buy, and use vitamins and dietary supplements as part of their wellness routines.

## About this survey

This survey screens for whether and how long respondents take supplements and which products they use, then measures behaviors such as how many they take, monthly spending, reasons for taking them, what prompted them to start, where they buy, brand versus store-brand choices, and preferred forms. It covers how choices are made, adherence to label directions, and whether their doctor knows, and includes agreement batteries on value for money, perceived benefit, importance to their routine, trust in label claims, and store-brand quality. An importance matrix rates price, brand, third-party certification, ingredients, and reviews, followed by subscription buying, reasons for stopping, a top-three ranking of priorities, and closing satisfaction, likelihood to recommend, and intent to continue. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking, and numeric free-text items. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, marital status, region, community type, and overall health orientation.
