# Home Health Devices

Consumer ownership, usage, and attitudes toward at-home health measurement devices.

## About this survey

This survey covers which home health devices respondents own (blood pressure monitors, thermometers, pulse oximeters, smart scales, glucose meters, heart-rate wearables), how long and how often they use them, their main reasons, brands owned (Omron, Withings, iHealth, Braun, Kinsa), where they were purchased, device count, and recent spend. It measures app connectivity, how readings are tracked and shared with providers, and who sets devices up, then runs Likert batteries on agreement (accuracy, ease, control, value, motivation, data security) and on the importance of purchase factors, a top-3 feature ranking, confidence, trust versus a clinic, concerns, satisfaction, a 0–10 recommendation score, and future purchase and usage intent, plus an open comment. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, a matrix battery split per item, a top-3 ranking, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured: age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, urban/rural, insurance status, and household condition management.
