# Symptom Checker Apps

How consumers use, trust, and evaluate online symptom checkers and health self-assessment tools.

## About this survey

This survey explores consumer use of online symptom checkers and health self-assessment tools such as WebMD, Mayo Clinic, Ada, Buoy Health, and AI chatbots. It begins with screener questions on awareness and usage frequency, then measures which tools and devices people use, who they check symptoms for, what prompts use, and what actions they take afterward. Likert batteries capture perceived usefulness, trust, reassurance versus worry, and ease of use, an importance battery rates desired features, and a top-3 ranking identifies reasons for use. Additional questions address perceived accuracy, effect on care-seeking, privacy concerns, comparison with web search and AI chatbots, willingness to pay, satisfaction, likelihood to recommend, and future intent. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, community type, tech comfort, and insurance status. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several rating scales, a matrix battery, a ranking item, and numeric free-text.
