# Treasury Bond Investing

How people invest in U.S. Treasury securities — bills, notes, bonds, TIPS, and savings bonds — for safety, income, and diversification.

## About this survey

This survey screens for familiarity with Treasury securities and current ownership, then measures awareness and holdings across T-bills, notes, bonds, TIPS, and I and EE savings bonds, along with how respondents buy them (TreasuryDirect, brokerages, funds, advisors) and their reasons for investing. Free-text items capture amount invested and attractive yield. Likert batteries assess attitudes about safety, understanding of rate-price dynamics, portfolio role, inflation worries, and perceived complexity, with an importance matrix on investing factors (safety, yield, inflation protection, liquidity, tax advantages) and a top-3 ranking of reasons to invest. Additional items cover preferred maturities, I bond limits, maturity decisions, laddering, risk tolerance, bond allocation, information sources, attention to Federal Reserve decisions, likelihood to buy, satisfaction, a 0–10 recommendation score, and an optional comment. A comprehensive demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, education, employment, income, marital status, investable assets, life stage, region, and urbanicity.
