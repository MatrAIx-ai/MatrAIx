# Screen Time Tracking

How people track, monitor, and manage their screen time and digital habits.

## About this survey

This survey opens with a screener on whether the respondent tracks screen time, which tools they use (Apple Screen Time, Digital Wellbeing, third-party apps, router controls), how often they check stats, and whether they set limits, then details the management features they use, the devices they monitor, their motivations, and their most-used app category. Attitudes are measured with an agreement battery (overuse, tracking effectiveness, bypassable limits, phone anxiety, guilt, satisfaction, and social media as a time sink), an importance matrix for reasons to manage screen time, and a top-three ranking of effective strategies, plus questions on activities to cut down, wind-down routines, and year-over-year change. Outcome items cover satisfaction with digital balance, self-control confidence, reduction intent, and likelihood to recommend tracking, using single-choice, multi-select, Likert, matrix, ranking, and numeric free-text formats. The demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, relationship status, children in the household, region, urban/suburban/rural setting, mobile platform, and overall tech comfort.
