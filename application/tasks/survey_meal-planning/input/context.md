# Meal Planning

How people plan, shop for, and prepare their meals week to week.

## About this survey

This survey measures how often and how far ahead people plan meals, the methods and apps or services they use (recipe managers, Mealime, grocery apps, meal kits such as HelloFresh, prepared-meal delivery), grocery-list and shopping habits, and how many days they cook at home. It captures meal-prep practices, reasons for planning, weekly grocery spend, and where people find recipes. Attitudes are measured through agreement and importance batteries covering saving money, healthy eating, stress, food waste, variety, and plan adherence, along with challenges, plan-failure fallbacks, food-waste frequency, and meal-kit experience. It closes with satisfaction, intent, a top-three needs ranking, and a likelihood-to-recommend score. A comprehensive demographic block captures age band, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, children in the household, who cooks, marital status, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural setting, and self-rated health.
