# Console Gaming

How people play video games on game consoles such as PlayStation, Xbox, and Nintendo Switch, including ownership, spending, and what drives their console choice.

## About this survey

This survey covers console gaming behavior: which consoles respondents own, their main console, weekly hours, and library size. It measures preferred genres through a top-3 ranking, physical-versus-digital buying, how games are acquired, yearly spending, and subscription use, along with play and usage behaviors (online and local multiplayer, voice chat, DLC, media streaming, microtransactions) through a frequency battery. Attitudes toward gaming as a hobby, console convenience, exclusives, physical ownership, and subscription value are measured through Likert scales, and an importance battery covers console-choice drivers, alongside trade-in behavior, plans to buy a new console, who people play with, satisfaction, and future intent. The question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert and importance batteries, a ranking question, a 0–10 recommendation scale, and numeric free-text. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, presence of children, U.S. region, and urban/suburban/rural setting.
