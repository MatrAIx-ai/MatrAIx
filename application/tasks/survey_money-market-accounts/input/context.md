# Money Market Accounts

How people use money market accounts and money market funds to hold cash while earning interest and keeping easy access.

## About this survey

This survey screens for familiarity with money market accounts, whether respondents distinguish deposit accounts from money market funds, and current ownership, then covers where balances are held (banks, credit unions, brokerages), reasons for use, and account features such as check-writing, debit access, tiered rates, and insurance. Free-text items capture total balance, current APY, and minimum balance requirements. Likert batteries measure attitudes about safety, convenience, minimum-balance drawbacks, opportunity cost, and understanding of fund versus deposit risk, plus an importance matrix on choosing an account (rate, access, fees, minimum balance, insurance) and a top-3 ranking of reasons for use. Additional questions address withdrawal limits, emergency-fund use, decision criteria, information sources, likelihood to open or add funds, satisfaction, a 0–10 recommendation score, and an optional comment. A full demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, education, employment, income, marital status, household size, total savings and investments, region, and urbanicity.
