# Preventive Care Attitudes

Consumer attitudes toward preventive health care and how those attitudes translate into routine checkups, screenings, and healthy-lifestyle habits.

## About this survey

This survey measures whether respondents have a regular source of care, how recently and how often they seek preventive services, and what prompts or tracks their visits. It includes Likert batteries on beliefs about prevention and the importance of specific services (blood pressure, cholesterol, cancer screenings, dental, vaccinations, vision), plus questions on barriers such as cost, time, and worry about results. It also covers lifestyle prevention steps, confidence in knowing one's health numbers, satisfaction and quality ratings, a likelihood-to-recommend scale, and a top-3 ranking of what would encourage more preventive care. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several Likert scales, a matrix battery split per item, numeric free-text items, and a demographic block covering age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, area type, insurance, and self-rated health.
