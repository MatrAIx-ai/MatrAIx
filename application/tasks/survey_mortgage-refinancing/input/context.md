# Mortgage Refinancing

People's experiences with and attitudes toward refinancing a mortgage.

## About this survey

This survey examines whether respondents have a mortgage and their experience with refinancing — recent refinancers, considerers, or those who have never looked into it — along with reasons (lower rate, lower payment, shorter term, cash out, dropping PMI), refinance type, current rate, and what has held them back. It measures lender comparison, break-even analysis, how options were discovered, rate-trend attention, and the rate drop that would prompt action. Likert batteries capture attitudes toward savings, complexity, understanding of closing costs, rate-watching, and equity risk, plus the importance of rate, payment, costs, tenure, and cash out, while multi-select and top-3 items cover reasons, barriers, lender types, information sources, and decision factors. A 0–10 confidence scale and numeric free-text record remaining balance, closing costs, and monthly payment, and a full demographic block captures age, gender, race or ethnicity, income, education, employment, self-reported credit score range, years of ownership, household size, marital status, region, and community type.
