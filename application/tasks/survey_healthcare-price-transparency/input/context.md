# Healthcare Price Transparency

Consumer behavior around, and attitudes toward, knowing the price of healthcare before receiving it.

## About this survey

This survey measures how often people try to find out what care will cost, which services they have checked prices for (visits, labs, imaging, medications, procedures, dental/vision), and which sources they use (insurer tools, hospital price lists, comparison apps, billing offices, prescription-price tools). It covers how easy prices were to find, how accurate estimates proved, whether respondents switched providers, delayed care, asked for estimates, or negotiated bills, and their awareness of hospital price-posting rules. Attitudes are captured through agreement and importance Likert batteries covering information adequacy, difficulty, influence on choices, trust, support for required disclosure, price-quality beliefs, and importance by care type, plus a "top 3" priorities ranking, barriers, tool preferences, overall satisfaction, a 0–10 likelihood scale, and future intent. Numeric free-text items capture lookups per year, time spent, a bill-tolerance threshold, and household size, followed by a comprehensive demographic block (age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, marital status, household size, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural, insurance type, and deductible level).
