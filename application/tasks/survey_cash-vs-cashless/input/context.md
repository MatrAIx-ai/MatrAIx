# Cash vs. Cashless Payments

How people balance physical cash and cashless payment methods (cards, mobile wallets, and peer-to-peer apps) in everyday life.

## About this survey

This survey screens for how often respondents use cash, their most-used everyday payment method, and the share of spending done in cash (free-text), then captures which methods they use regularly, how cash use has changed, and the situations where they still prefer cash. Free-text items record cash typically carried and ATM habits. Likert batteries measure attitudes about going cashless, spending control, convenience, privacy, financial inclusion, and security, plus an importance matrix on payment-method factors (convenience, security, rewards, budgeting, privacy) and two top-3 rankings for reasons to prefer cashless and to keep using cash. Additional items cover cashless-only experiences and opinions, mobile wallet apps and usage, security concerns, emergency cash, and expected future cash use, ending with overall sentiment about a cashless society, satisfaction, a 0–10 readiness scale, and an optional comment. A comprehensive demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, education, employment, income, marital status, household size, banking status, region, and urbanicity.
