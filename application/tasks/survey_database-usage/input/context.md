# Database Usage

How developers and data professionals choose, use, and manage databases.

## About this survey

This survey profiles how people work with databases, starting with a screener on frequency, tenure, and the respondent's role. It records the primary database along with relational and NoSQL systems used and wanted, how respondents interact with databases, the GUI clients and ORMs they rely on, hosting model, managed services, and SQL proficiency. Attitudes are captured with an agreement Likert battery on SQL confidence, NoSQL fit, tuning, and security and a per-item importance matrix over database-selection criteria, plus a top-three ranking of decision factors and a multi-select of database tasks performed. Additional sections cover performance problems, backups, migrations, data volume, caching, and satisfaction with performance and tooling, followed by overall satisfaction, recommendation likelihood, adoption intent, and switching intent. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking item, and numeric free-text. The survey concludes with a demographic block covering role, professional experience, employment, company size, industry, age, gender, race and ethnicity, education, U.S. region, and urbanicity.
