# Wearable Heart Monitor

How consumers use wearable devices to track heart rate and heart health.

## About this survey

This survey examines consumer use of wearable devices that track heart rate, such as the Apple Watch, Fitbit, Garmin, Whoop, and Oura Ring. It begins with screener questions on device usage, type, brand, tenure, and wear habits, then measures which heart metrics people track, their reasons for tracking, how often they review data, and where. Likert batteries capture attitudes toward accuracy, motivation, worry, and clarity, while an importance battery rates device features and change items assess effects on activity. A top-3 ranking identifies what people value most about heart-rate tracking, and further questions cover irregular-rhythm alerts, sharing data with providers, privacy concerns, trust, spending, satisfaction, likelihood to recommend, and future intent. A comprehensive demographic block captures age, gender, race and ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, community type, and activity level. Question types include single-choice, multi-select, several rating scales, a matrix battery, a ranking item, and numeric free-text.
