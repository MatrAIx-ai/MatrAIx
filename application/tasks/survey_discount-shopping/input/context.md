# Discount Shopping

How consumers shop for discounts, deals, and lower-priced goods across discount, off-price, warehouse, and secondhand channels.

## About this survey

This survey characterizes each respondent's overall price sensitivity and whether their focus on deals has grown over the past year. It maps the discount channels they use (dollar stores, warehouse clubs, off-price and outlet retailers, thrift, marketplaces, and resale apps), how often they buy secondhand, the categories they target, and the share of spending devoted to bargains. It captures how people judge a good deal and their tendency to wait for sales. Attitudes are measured through agreement batteries on bargain satisfaction, overspending, quality preference, secondhand value, and inflation pressure, and an importance battery on price, quality, convenience, brand, and sustainability. Additional items cover planned sale events, money-saving behaviors, downsides, a top-3 motivations ranking, inflation impact, satisfaction, future intent, and a 0-10 deal-hunting enjoyment scale, plus open numeric and text fields. It closes with a demographic block including age, gender, race or ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, community type, and perceived financial situation.
