# Payday and Short-Term Loan Usage

People's experiences with payday loans and other short-term, high-cost borrowing.

## About this survey

This survey screens for use of payday and other short-term loans (storefront and online payday, auto title, pawnshop, paycheck-advance apps, overdraft, buy-now-pay-later) and captures how many loans were taken, amounts borrowed, and fees paid. It asks what the money was used for, where the loan came from, why this option was chosen, how well the cost was understood, repayment and rollover behavior, and which alternatives were considered. Agreement batteries measure perceived speed, cost surprise, impact, necessity, and reuse intent, alongside items on financial fragility, banking and credit access, running short before payday, and attitudes toward regulation. A top-3 ranking identifies what would help respondents avoid short-term borrowing. It closes with future likelihood, satisfaction, and an open comment. A comprehensive demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, pay frequency, household size, marital status, housing tenure, region, and area type so personas can be matched.
