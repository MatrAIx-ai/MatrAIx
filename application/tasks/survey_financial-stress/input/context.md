# Financial Stress

How money worries affect people's lives and how they cope with financial stress.

## About this survey

This survey measures respondents' overall level of financial stress, how it has changed, and how often they worry about money. It covers ability to handle a surprise expense, the specific sources of stress (income, inflation, debt, housing, medical bills, job security), and hardships experienced in the past year. Agreement and frequency batteries gauge feelings of control and anxiety, including a matrix on how money worries affect sleep, mood, relationships, work, and physical health. It also captures emergency savings, debt burden, coping steps taken, who respondents turn to, avoidance behavior, and employer wellness resources, with numeric fields for accessible savings, debt-to-income share, and stressful days, plus a top-3 ranking of what would most help. It closes with confidence in handling emergencies, financial outlook, satisfaction, a 0-10 stress score, and an open comment. A comprehensive demographic block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, dependents, marital status, housing tenure, region, and area type so personas can be matched.
