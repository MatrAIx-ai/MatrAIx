# Student Loan Repayment

How borrowers manage, repay, and feel about their student loan debt.

## About this survey

This survey examines student loan repayment from balances and loan types through day-to-day repayment behavior. It records whose education was funded, the degree pursued, total and original balances, monthly payment, interest rate, servicer, repayment plan, and current status. It measures behaviors such as autopay, missed payments, extra payments, forgiveness-program participation, refinancing, and use of deferment or forbearance, then uses agreement and importance Likert batteries to capture attitudes about burden, regret, value, and payoff confidence. Sub-topics include stress, the effect on retirement saving, and views on forgiveness policy, followed by servicer satisfaction, payoff likelihood, a 0-to-10 recommendation score, a delayed-life-decisions ranking, and an optional comment. A full demographic block captures age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural setting, and homeownership.
