# Financial Emergency Preparedness

How ready households are to absorb unexpected expenses and income shocks, including their emergency savings, resources, and confidence.

## About this survey

This survey opens with the classic $400-expense screener and questions on whether respondents hold an emergency fund, how many months of expenses it covers, where the money is held, and how regularly they contribute. It records recent unexpected expenses and whether savings or debt were used to meet them. Attitudes are measured through agreement batteries on worry, stability, and saving priorities, and a preparedness battery spanning job loss, medical bills, car and home repairs, and natural disasters. It also captures backup resources, saving barriers, a top-3 preparedness-actions ranking, whether a plan exists, income stability, debt load, satisfaction, future saving intent, a 0-10 preparedness scale, and open numeric and text fields on current and target savings. It closes with a comprehensive demographic block including age, gender, race or ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, community type, homeownership, and number of children.
