# Diet Tracking Apps

Consumer use of smartphone apps to log food, calories, and nutrition.

## About this survey

This survey measures whether respondents use a diet-tracking app, for how long, and which ones (MyFitnessPal, Lose It!, Cronometer, Noom, WeightWatchers, and others), including their main app, goals, and how they log food. It captures logging frequency and completeness, the metrics people follow, subscription use, and monthly spend. Attitudes are measured through agreement, satisfaction, and importance batteries covering goal progress, ease and speed of logging, database accuracy, awareness, tracking fatigue, number-focus, privacy, insights, barcode scanning, value, and desired features, plus device integration, barriers, churn, retention intent, a likelihood-to-recommend score, and a top-three improvements ranking. A comprehensive demographic block captures age band, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, marital status, U.S. region, urban/suburban/rural setting, wearable ownership, and self-rated health.
