# Workflow Automation Tools

How people use no-code and low-code workflow automation tools to connect apps and automate repetitive tasks — which tools they choose, what they automate, and how well it works.

## About this survey

This survey covers adoption and use of workflow automation platforms such as Zapier, Make, Microsoft Power Automate, IFTTT, and n8n. It measures how long and how often people build automations, how many they run and the time they save, the tasks they automate and the apps they connect, their technical background and building approach, and use of AI features. Attitude batteries capture agreement about time savings, reliability, and pricing, an importance rating of tool-selection factors, and a top-three priority ranking. Later sections cover frustrations, failure detection, learning methods, satisfaction, likelihood to recommend, switching intent, and future reliance. Demographics captured include age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, role, organization size, region, and area type.
