# Peer-to-Peer Payment Apps

How people use peer-to-peer payment apps such as Venmo, Cash App, Zelle, and PayPal to send and receive money, and their attitudes toward safety and privacy.

## About this survey

This survey screens for P2P app use and tenure, then captures which apps respondents use, their most-used app, frequency, monthly send volume, use cases, who they exchange money with, funding sources, and balance habits. It records extra features (spending cards, direct deposit, investing, savings), decision drivers through an importance battery (ease, network, speed, security, fees), attitudes via agreement scales, and a concern battery covering mistaken payments, scams, hacking, and transaction visibility. Additional sections cover past problems, a top-3 ranking of reasons for use, safety confidence, satisfaction, future intent, and a likelihood-to-recommend scale, with numeric free-text for monthly volume and household size. A comprehensive demographics block records age, gender, race/ethnicity, income, education, employment, household size, marital status, region, community type, smartphone type, bank account access, and technology comfort.
