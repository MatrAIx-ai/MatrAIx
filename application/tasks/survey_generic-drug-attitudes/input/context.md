# Generic Drug Attitudes

Consumers' beliefs about, comfort with, and use of generic versus brand-name medications.

## About this survey

This survey screens for medication use and default choice between generics and brand names, then measures familiarity, overall sentiment, specific beliefs about generics, and where respondents get their information. It probes behaviors such as requesting generics or brand names and includes agreement batteries on effectiveness, safety, willingness to pay more, savings, trust in the approval process, and noticing differences after switching. Comfort questions cover routine generic use and unannounced substitution, and an importance matrix rates price, doctor and pharmacist input, and brand familiarity in the decision. Later questions cover condition-specific preferences, the savings threshold that would prompt a switch, self-assessed knowledge, and a top-three ranking of what would build comfort, before closing satisfaction and likelihood to recommend. Question types include single-choice, multi- select, several Likert scales, an importance matrix, a ranking, and numeric free-text items. Demographics captured include age, gender, race or ethnicity, household income, education, employment status, household size, marital status, region, community type, and primary insurance source.
